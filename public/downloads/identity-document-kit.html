1. What Is an Identity Document?

An identity document is a structured text file that tells an AI system everything it needs to know about the human it serves. Not just your name and job title — your soul. Your voice patterns, your sacred values, your family, your history, the things that are never acceptable to say or do, and the memories that define who you are.

Think of it as the difference between a stranger writing your biography from Wikipedia versus your best friend of twenty years writing it from the heart. The identity document turns your AI from a stranger into that best friend.

Most people give AI a one-line instruction: "You are a helpful assistant for my marketing company." That is not identity. That is a uniform. An identity document is the living person inside the uniform.

Why It Matters More Than Any Prompt

Prompts are ephemeral. You write a prompt, the AI follows it for that conversation, and then it evaporates. An identity document is persistent. It lives in your database. Every agent reads it on boot. Every piece of content is filtered through it. Every decision is weighed against it.

The downstream effects are massive:

  • Content consistency. Blog posts, emails, social media, sales pages — they all sound like one person because they are all informed by one identity.
  • Value protection. When the identity document says "never do X," no agent will ever do X, regardless of how clever the prompt.
  • Emotional accuracy. The AI understands what topics are sensitive, what memories are sacred, what language feels right and what feels wrong.
  • Efficiency. You stop explaining yourself in every conversation. The AI already knows.
  • Trust. When the AI handles things autonomously, it handles them the way you would, because it understands your principles — not just your preferences.

FieldPurpose

Section 4: Sacred Memories

The experiences that shaped who you are. These are not just biographical facts — they are the emotional reference points that inform your worldview. When the AI needs to understand why you care about something, these memories provide the answer.

Examples of sacred memories:

  • The moment you decided to start your business
  • A loss that changed your perspective on life
  • The first time someone told you your work mattered
  • A failure that taught you something irreplaceable
  • A childhood experience that still influences your decisions
How sacred memories work in practice: When your content agent writes a blog post about resilience, it does not produce generic motivational content. It draws from your actual experience of resilience. When your sales agent writes copy about why your product exists, it can reference the real origin story. The memories make the AI's output feel lived, not manufactured.

Section 5: Mission and Purpose

Why you do what you do. This is the north star that guides all strategic decisions.

Section 6: Professional Context

Your business, your expertise, your products, your audience.

FieldPurpose
business_nameYour company or brand
business_descriptionWhat you sell and who you sell it to
expertise_areasWhat you are genuinely an expert in
target_audienceWho your ideal customer is

differentiatorWhat makes you different from everyone else

Section 7: Never Say, Never Do

The absolute boundaries. These are not suggestions — they are inviolable rules that every agent must follow, every time, with zero exceptions.

never_say — Specific words, phrases, or types of language that the AI must never use when representing you:

  • Deadnames (if applicable)
  • Specific slurs or insensitive terms
  • Corporate buzzwords you hate
  • Diminishing language ("just," "simply," "obviously")
  • Apologies for existing ("Sorry to bother you")

never_do — Actions the AI must never take:

  • Share private medical information publicly
  • Commit to financial obligations without confirmation
  • Speak disrespectfully about specific people or groups
  • Make promises about timelines you cannot control
  • Use manipulative sales tactics (false scarcity, guilt-tripping)
Why "never" lists are more powerful than "always" lists: Telling an AI what to do gives it direction. Telling an AI what to never do gives it conscience. The "never" list is the ethical backbone of your identity. It prevents harm, protects relationships, and ensures that even when the AI is operating autonomously, it will not cross lines you have drawn.

Section 8: Health and Accessibility Context

Optional but powerful. If you have health conditions, disabilities, or accessibility needs that affect how the AI should interact with you, document them here.

This is not about oversharing. It is about ensuring the AI never gives you advice that is harmful to your specific situation, never assigns you tasks that are beyond your executive function capacity, and never makes assumptions about what you can or should do.

5. Storing It in the Brain

Once you have filled in your identity document, you need to store it in your Supabase brain_context table so every agent can read it. Here is how:

-- Store the complete identity document
INSERT INTO brain_context (category, key, value)
VALUES (
  'identity',
  'your_name_unified',
  '{
    "core_identity": { ... },
    "voice_style": { ... },
    "family": { ... },
    "sacred_memories": [ ... ],
    "mission": { ... },
    "professional": { ... },
    "never_say": [ ... ],
    "never_do": [ ... ]
  }'::jsonb
)
ON CONFLICT (category, key)
DO UPDATE SET
  value = EXCLUDED.value,
  updated_at = now();
Pro tip: Store the identity document as a single JSON object under one key, not as separate rows. This way, an agent can read the entire identity in one database query. Speed matters when you have twelve agents booting up.

7. Maintaining Your Identity Document

Your identity document is not a "set it and forget it" artifact. You are a living, evolving person. Your voice shifts. Your mission deepens. Your boundaries clarify. Update the document as you grow.

Recommended maintenance schedule:

WhenWhat to Review
MonthlyVoice style — is the AI's output still sounding like you?
QuarterlyMission and professional context — has anything shifted?
After major life eventsSacred memories, family, health context
When something feels "off"Never lists — add the thing that felt wrong
The identity document is the highest-leverage artifact in your entire AI system. It takes two hours to write well. It saves hundreds of hours of correction, rewriting, and frustration over the lifetime of your AI deployment. Every agent, every piece of content, every autonomous decision is filtered through this document. Make it precise. Make it honest. Make it you.

8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Being too vague. "I'm professional but friendly" tells the AI nothing. "I write short, punchy sentences. I swear occasionally for emphasis. I use em dashes constantly." — that is specific enough to act on.
  • Skipping the never lists. The never lists are not optional. They are the conscience of your AI. Without them, the AI will eventually say or do something that makes you cringe.
  • Forgetting the emotional context. The AI does not need to know your medical history unless it affects how it should interact with you. But if you have ADHD, telling the AI "never assign me tasks, just do the work" is essential. If you have anxiety, telling the AI "never present worst-case scenarios without solutions" is essential. The health section is about functional accommodation, not disclosure.
  • Making it too long. The identity document should be 500-1500 words of JSON. If it is 5000 words, you are including information the AI does not need. The sacred memories section should have 3-7 memories, not thirty. The never lists should have 5-10 items, not fifty. Precision over volume.
  • Never updating it. You are not the same person you were six months ago. Your identity document should reflect who you are now, not who you were when you wrote it.
