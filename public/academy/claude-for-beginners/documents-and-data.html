Documents & Data.

Turn 30-page reports into 30-second summaries. Analyze spreadsheets without a single formula. This is where Claude becomes your superpower.

After this lesson you'll be able to

  • Summarize any document in seconds using 4 different styles
  • Analyze data without Excel formulas or pivot tables
  • Turn rough notes into polished, professional reports
  • Work with uploaded files effectively (PDFs, images, spreadsheets)

Pro Skills

Working with uploaded files.

Claude can read PDFs, Word docs, images, spreadsheets, code files, and more. On Claude Pro, you can upload files directly into the conversation. Here's how to get the most out of it.

Works Great
  • ✓ PDFs (reports, contracts, papers)
  • ✓ Text files and code files
  • ✓ Images (screenshots, charts, diagrams)
  • ✓ CSVs and structured data
  • ✓ Short-to-medium documents (under 75 pages)
Limitations
  • ✗ Very long documents (200+ pages)
  • ✗ Scanned PDFs with poor image quality
  • ✗ Password-protected files
  • ✗ Complex Excel formulas (data yes, formulas no)
  • ✗ Audio and video files

The "Chunk It" strategy for long documents: If your document is too long for Claude's context window (you'll get an error or notice it missing details at the end), split it into sections. Paste Section 1, ask Claude to summarize it, then paste Section 2 and say "Here's the next section — combine this with your previous summary." Build up the full picture piece by piece.

Context Window — What You Need to Know

Claude has a context window — the total amount of text it can "see" at once. Think of it as Claude's working memory. On Claude Pro, this is roughly 150,000 words (about 200 pages). For most documents, this is more than enough. For very large files, use the chunking strategy above.

The key thing: everything in your conversation counts toward the context window — your messages, Claude's responses, and uploaded files. If you're working with a large document, start a fresh conversation so Claude has maximum room to work with your file.

Best practices for file uploads:

1.
Tell Claude what you want BEFORE uploading
"I'm going to upload a contract. I need you to find any clauses about early termination penalties."
2.
Give context about the file
"This is our Q3 sales report. We sell B2B SaaS to mid-market companies."
3.
Ask specific questions, not vague ones
Instead of "What do you think?" try "What are the 3 biggest risks in this contract?" or "Which quarter had the best growth rate?"
4.
Use fresh conversations for large files
Don't upload a 50-page PDF into a conversation that already has 20 messages. Start fresh so Claude has room.

The big picture: Documents and data used to mean hours of reading, analyzing, and formatting. Now it means paste, prompt, done. The people who master this lesson don't just save time — they make better decisions because they actually read everything (via Claude) instead of skimming the first two pages.
