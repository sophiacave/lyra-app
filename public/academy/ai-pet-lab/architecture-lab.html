Architecture Matters

1 neuron: Can only learn the simplest trick (sit). Not enough capacity for complex behaviors. Like trying to memorize a book with a post-it note.

2-3 neurons: Can handle basic tricks. Good balance of capacity and training speed. Like a small notebook — enough for daily tasks.

4-5 neurons: Can learn all tricks including complex ones. But takes longer to train and might overfit (memorize the training examples so precisely that it fails on new situations, like a student who memorizes test answers but can't solve new problems). Like having a library — powerful but needs more organization.

This mirrors real neural networks: more parameters (the individual numbers the network adjusts during training -- each connection between neurons is one parameter) = more capacity (the range of patterns the network can represent) but slower training and risk of overfitting. The art is finding the right size for your problem.
