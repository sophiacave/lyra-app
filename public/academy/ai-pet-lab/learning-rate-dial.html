Learning Rate Explained

The learning rate controls how big each learning step is. Think of it as how dramatically your pet changes its behavior after each reward.

Too high (near 1.0): The pet overreacts to every signal. It swings wildly between behaviors — the loss oscillates and never converges. Like a pet that completely changes personality after every treat!

Too low (near 0.001): The pet barely changes. Training takes forever. The loss creeps down agonizingly slowly. Like a pet that needs 10,000 treats to learn one trick.

Just right (0.01-0.1): Smooth, steady descent toward the optimal behavior. The sweet spot depends on the problem — that's why it's the most important hyperparameter to tune!
