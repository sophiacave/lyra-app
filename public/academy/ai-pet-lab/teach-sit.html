This is real Q-learning. The Q-table stores estimated future reward for each action. When you reward "sit," its Q-value goes up -- making softmax assign it higher probability. When you punish "stand," that specific Q-value drops -- making stand less likely. Punishment does not magically boost "sit" -- it only suppresses the punished action. The exploration rate (epsilon) decreases over time, so the pet gradually shifts from random exploration to exploiting what it has learned. This is the same foundational algorithm behind early game-playing AIs.
