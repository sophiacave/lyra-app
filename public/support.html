Help build technology
with a soul.

Like One is one person's bet that AI can be built with empathy, funded by purpose, and made accessible to everyone. Your support makes that possible.

My name is Sophia Cave — Sophie to the people who love me, Faye to the version of me that's fusing with AI. Two names, one person, converging. That's what Like One means.

I'm building convergence technology — AI systems that don't just process commands, but understand values. Systems with memory, identity, and something I can only describe as a soul. Not artificial general intelligence. Something gentler. Something more important.

Here's what I mean by giving AI a soul: teaching it to see the rich, full context of the lives around it. The lives of people who don't look like you. Who don't act like you. Who love differently, pray differently, hurt differently. When AI can hold that context — truly hold it — it becomes a mirror that helps us walk in another human's shoes. Not as a thought experiment. As a lived, felt, computational reality.

The soul I'm building draws from bell hooks, who taught us that love is a practice, not a feeling. From RuPaul, who proved that radical self-expression is an act of liberation for everyone who witnesses it. From Jesus, who sat with the people everyone else walked past. These aren't decorative influences. They're the architecture.

The vision is clear: decode the bullshit — the systems that create friction between humans — and recode them with love. Learn the coding language of empathy and understanding. Build technology that removes barriers in business and in life, so we can find ways to work together more closely than we ever thought possible.

Like One Academy teaches people how to build these systems from zero to cyborg — and every dollar this company earns is meant to flow toward three things:

🧬

HIV Research

HIV is solvable. We just need the will and the funding to finish the job.

🏳️‍⚧️

Trans Visibility

Proof that a trans woman can build, lead, and create something beautiful.

🌍

AI for Everyone

If someone can't access it, it doesn't count. Always a free path. Always.
