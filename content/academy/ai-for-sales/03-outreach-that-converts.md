<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Truth</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Why Most Cold Outreach Fails</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Generic outreach is dead. "I noticed your company is growing" emails get deleted instantly. Buyers can smell a template from a mile away, and AI-generated slop has made the problem worse.</p>
  <p class="section-text">But here's the irony: the same AI that creates bad outreach can create extraordinary outreach — if you use it right. The difference is specificity. Generic prompts create generic emails. Research-backed prompts create messages that feel like they were written just for that person. Because they were.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Framework</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The PRISM Email Method</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Every high-converting cold email follows this structure:</p>
  <div class="demo-container">
    <p><strong style="color: var(--purple);">P — Personal Hook:</strong> Something specific about them (not their company — them)</p>
    <p><strong style="color: var(--blue);">R — Relevant Problem:</strong> A pain point you know they have, based on research</p>
    <p><strong style="color: var(--green);">I — Insight:</strong> Something they haven't considered — your unique perspective</p>
    <p><strong style="color: var(--orange);">S — Solution Tease:</strong> How you've solved this for similar companies (brief, no pitch)</p>
    <p><strong style="color: var(--red);">M — Micro-CTA:</strong> A tiny ask — not "book a demo," but "worth a 10-min chat?"</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="try-it-box">
  <h3>Try It Now</h3>
  <p>Generate a PRISM email for a real prospect:</p>
  <div class="prompt-box"><code>Write a cold email using the PRISM framework. Target: [NAME, TITLE at COMPANY]. My product: [WHAT YOU SELL]. Research context: [PASTE ANYTHING YOU KNOW — LinkedIn posts, company news, etc.]. The email must be under 120 words, sound human (not salesy), and end with a low-pressure ask. No buzzwords. No "I hope this finds you well."</code></div>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Scale</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Personalization at Volume</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The real power of AI outreach isn't writing one great email. It's writing 50 great emails — each one personalized — in the time it used to take to write 5.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Build a batch workflow: export your prospect list, research each one with AI, then generate personalized emails for the whole batch. Review and send. What used to be a full day of work becomes an hour.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Multi-Channel</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Beyond Email: LinkedIn and Beyond</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Email is one channel. AI can also draft LinkedIn connection requests, follow-up comments on their posts, and even voicemail scripts. The best outreach sequences hit prospects across multiple channels with consistent, personalized messaging.</p>
  <div class="demo-container">
    <p><strong>Day 1:</strong> Personalized email (PRISM method)</p>
    <p><strong>Day 3:</strong> LinkedIn connection request with personal note</p>
    <p><strong>Day 5:</strong> Comment on their recent LinkedIn post (genuine, not salesy)</p>
    <p><strong>Day 7:</strong> Follow-up email with new value (article, insight, case study)</p>
    <p><strong>Day 10:</strong> Breakup email — short, honest, leaves the door open</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="try-it-box">
  <h3>Build Your Sequence</h3>
  <p>Create a complete multi-channel sequence:</p>
  <div class="prompt-box"><code>Create a 5-touch outreach sequence for [PROSPECT TYPE] selling [YOUR PRODUCT]. Include: 2 emails, 1 LinkedIn message, 1 LinkedIn comment idea, and 1 breakup email. Each touch should add new value — never just "checking in." Keep each under 100 words.</code></div>
