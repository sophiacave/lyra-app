<div class="lesson-hero">
  <h1>Launch and Distribution</h1>
  <p><span class="accent">Nobody cares about your product until you make them care.</span></p>
  <p>Building is 30% of the work. Getting it in front of the right people is the other 70%. AI products have unique distribution advantages — if you know how to use them.</p>
</div>

    <li>Building in public as a distribution engine</li>
  </ul>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Strategy</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The Three-Wave Launch</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Wave 1 — Inner circle (Week -2):</strong> Give your product to 20-50 people you know. Friends, colleagues, Twitter mutuals. Ask them to break it. This isn't about growth — it's about catching embarrassing failures before strangers see them.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Wave 2 — Community seeding (Week 0):</strong> Post in the communities where your target users already gather. Product Hunt, Hacker News, relevant subreddits, Discord servers, LinkedIn groups. Don't post "check out my AI tool." Post the output. Show the magic trick.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Wave 3 — Content flywheel (Week 1+):</strong> Turn your product's outputs into content. Screenshots of results, before/after comparisons, user testimonials, use case breakdowns. Every piece of content demonstrates the product without being an ad.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Tactic</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Show the Output, Not the Tool</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI products have a superpower that traditional SaaS doesn't: the output is inherently shareable. A beautifully generated design, a perfectly structured summary, a surprisingly accurate analysis — these are their own marketing.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Add a subtle watermark or "made with [your product]" tag on outputs. Make sharing frictionless — one-click export to social media, email-ready formats, embeddable widgets. Every output your user shares is a free ad that demonstrates value instead of claiming it.</p>
</div>

<div class="demo-container">
  <h3>Distribution Channels Ranked for AI Products</h3>
  <p><strong style="color: var(--green);">High ROI:</strong> Twitter/X (AI community is huge), Product Hunt, niche subreddits, YouTube demos</p>
  <p><strong style="color: var(--orange);">Medium ROI:</strong> LinkedIn (for B2B), Hacker News, Discord communities, SEO/blog content</p>
  <p><strong style="color: var(--red);">Low ROI (for now):</strong> Paid ads (CAC too high for early stage), cold email, influencer partnerships</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Method</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Building in Public</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Document your journey building the AI product. Share your architecture decisions, your prompt engineering insights, your cost breakdowns. The AI builder community is hungry for real, transparent content. Every post attracts potential users who think "if they're this thoughtful about building it, the product must be good."</p>
  <p class="section-text">Share numbers. "We hit 100 users this week. Average query cost dropped from $0.08 to $0.03 after prompt optimization. Here's how." This kind of content builds trust, attracts users, and establishes authority simultaneously.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Reality</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Launch Day Is Just Day One</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Most AI products don't go viral on launch day. They grow through consistent demonstration of value over weeks and months. The Product Hunt launch gets you 500 visitors. The Twitter thread that shows a mind-blowing output gets you 5,000. The SEO article that ranks for "best AI tool for [your niche]" gets you 500 visitors every month forever.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Plan for sustained distribution, not a single moment. The launch is a starting gun, not the finish line.</p>
</div>
