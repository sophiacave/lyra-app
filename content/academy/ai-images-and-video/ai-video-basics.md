<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The State of AI Video</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">AI video is where AI images were two years ago: early, exciting, and evolving fast.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">If AI image generation felt like magic the first time you tried it, AI video generation will feel like sorcery. You describe a scene or upload an image, and the tool generates a video clip — complete with motion, camera movement, and lighting changes. The clips are short (typically 4-16 seconds) but the quality has improved dramatically in the last year.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Fair warning: AI video is not yet at the "looks completely real" stage for everything. But for social media clips, creative projects, concept visualization, and artistic expression, it is already genuinely useful.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Major Tools</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Here is your map to the AI video landscape.</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Runway Gen-3:</strong> The most established AI video platform. Offers text-to-video, image-to-video, and video-to-video transformation. Known for cinematic quality and good motion coherence. Starts at $12/month. This is the one most professionals reach for first.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Pika:</strong> Focuses on making AI video creation simple and fun. Excellent for quick social clips and creative experiments. Has a generous free tier. The interface is clean and beginner-friendly.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Kling AI:</strong> Produces impressive motion quality and handles complex scenes well. Known for longer generation times but higher quality results. Growing rapidly in popularity.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Sora (by OpenAI):</strong> Generates remarkably coherent and realistic video. Available through ChatGPT Plus/Pro. Excels at understanding physics and natural motion. Higher-tier plans get more generations.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Luma Dream Machine:</strong> Fast generation, good at dreamy and artistic styles. Free tier available. Particularly strong at image-to-video — turning still images into moving scenes.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Two Approaches</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Text-to-video versus image-to-video. Both are powerful, for different reasons.</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Text-to-video:</strong> You describe a scene in words and the AI generates the entire video from scratch. This gives you maximum creative freedom but less control over the exact look. It is best for when you want to explore ideas quickly.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Image-to-video:</strong> You upload a still image and the AI animates it — adding motion, camera movement, and life. This gives you much more control over the visual result because you start with an image you already like. Generate your perfect image first, then bring it to life with video.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The image-to-video approach is often the most practical workflow: use your AI image skills from earlier lessons to create the perfect starting frame, then use a video tool to animate it.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Your First AI Video</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Let's create one right now.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The simplest path is Pika or Luma Dream Machine, both of which have free tiers. Here is the process:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>1.</strong> Go to <a href="https://pika.art" target="_blank">pika.art</a> and sign up (free)<br>
  <strong>2.</strong> Click "Create" and choose text-to-video<br>
  <strong>3.</strong> Enter a simple prompt: "A cup of coffee with steam rising, on a wooden table by a rainy window, cozy morning light, slow gentle motion"<br>
  <strong>4.</strong> Generate and wait (usually 30-90 seconds)<br>
  <strong>5.</strong> Watch your video. Notice how the steam moves, how the light plays. That is AI video.</p>
</div>

<div class="demo-container" style="border-left: 3px solid var(--purple); padding: 1.2rem; margin: 2rem 0; background: var(--bg);">
  <h3 style="color: var(--purple);">Video prompt tips</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Describe motion explicitly:</strong> "slow pan across," "gentle zoom in," "camera orbiting around"</li>
    <li><strong>Keep it simple:</strong> One subject, one action, one mood. Complex scenes with multiple moving parts often struggle.</li>
    <li><strong>Specify camera movement:</strong> "static camera" vs "dolly shot" vs "tracking shot" makes a huge difference</li>
    <li><strong>Include atmosphere:</strong> "fog," "rain," "dust particles in light" — these atmospheric elements look beautiful in AI video</li>
  </ul>
</div>

  <p>Create an AI image you like using the prompt skills from previous lessons. Then take that image to an AI video tool (Pika, Luma, or Runway) and animate it using image-to-video. Add a motion prompt like <strong>"slow cinematic camera push in, subtle movement"</strong> and watch your still image come to life. Save the result — you just created your first AI video.</p>
