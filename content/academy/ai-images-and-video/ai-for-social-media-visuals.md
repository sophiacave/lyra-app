<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Social Media Problem</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Every platform demands visuals. AI makes that sustainable.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">If you run a business, a brand, or even a personal account, you know the pressure: Instagram wants beautiful squares and reels. LinkedIn wants polished headers. X (Twitter) wants eye-catching cards. Facebook wants everything. Creating fresh visual content for all of these used to require either design skills or a budget for a designer. AI changes that equation completely.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Building a Visual Brand</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Consistency is what separates a brand from random posts.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The secret to looking professional on social media is visual consistency. That means using the same color palette, similar styles, and a recognizable aesthetic across all your posts. AI makes this surprisingly easy once you find your brand formula.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Start by creating a "brand prompt" — a base description that captures your visual identity. For example:</p>
</div>

<div class="prompt-box" style="background: var(--dim); border-radius: 8px; padding: 1.2rem 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; font-family: monospace; font-size: 1.05rem;">
  [YOUR SUBJECT HERE], clean minimalist style, soft pastel color palette with coral and sage green accents, natural lighting, modern and warm feeling, lifestyle photography aesthetic
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Platform-Specific Sizes</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Knowing the right dimensions saves you from awkward cropping later.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">When you generate images, ask for specific aspect ratios:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Instagram feed:</strong> Square (1:1) or portrait (4:5) — the portrait format takes up more screen space, which is better for engagement<br>
  <strong>Instagram Stories / Reels:</strong> Vertical (9:16)<br>
  <strong>LinkedIn / X headers:</strong> Wide landscape (roughly 3:1)<br>
  <strong>YouTube thumbnails:</strong> Landscape (16:9)<br>
  <strong>Pinterest:</strong> Tall vertical (2:3)</p>
  <p class="section-text">Most AI tools let you specify aspect ratios directly. In Midjourney, add <code>--ar 4:5</code> at the end. In ChatGPT, just say "make it a vertical 4:5 ratio for Instagram."</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Batch Creating Content</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Create a week of content in thirty minutes.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Here is a workflow that works beautifully for weekly content:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>1. Plan your themes:</strong> Monday = tip, Wednesday = behind the scenes, Friday = inspiration<br>
  <strong>2. Write your brand prompt base</strong> (save it in a document so you can reuse it)<br>
  <strong>3. Swap out only the subject</strong> for each post while keeping the style consistent<br>
  <strong>4. Generate 2-3 options per post</strong> and pick the strongest<br>
  <strong>5. Add text overlays</strong> in Canva, Figma, or your preferred design tool</p>
  <p class="section-text">This batch approach means you sit down once, enter a creative flow, and walk away with a library of cohesive content. No more scrambling for a graphic an hour before posting.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Authenticity Matters</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">AI content works best when it feels personal, not stock.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The risk with AI-generated social content is that it can look generic — like stock photography but slightly off. Here is how to avoid that:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Be specific to your world:</strong> Instead of "a woman at a laptop," try "a freelance writer in a cozy home office with plants and warm lamp light, morning coffee steam visible." The details make it feel like a real moment.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Mix AI with real:</strong> Use AI for backgrounds, graphics, and illustrations, but include real photos of yourself, your products, or your space alongside them. The combination feels authentic.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Add your text and personality:</strong> An AI image with your words, your tone, and your perspective on top of it becomes uniquely yours. The image is the canvas; your voice is the art.</p>
</div>

<div class="try-it-box" style="border: 2px solid var(--green); border-radius: 8px; padding: 1.5rem; margin: 2rem 0; background: var(--bg);">
  <h3 style="color: var(--green);">Try it now</h3>
  <p>Write a brand prompt that describes your personal or business visual style. Then generate three social media images using that same base prompt with different subjects:</p>
  <ol>
    <li>A motivational quote background</li>
    <li>A "behind the scenes" lifestyle scene</li>
    <li>A product or service-related visual</li>
  </ol>
  <p>Look at all three together. Do they feel like they belong to the same brand? If yes, you have found your visual formula. If not, adjust the style keywords until they feel cohesive.</p>
</div>
