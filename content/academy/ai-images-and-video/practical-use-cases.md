<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Business Cards and Branding</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Create a visual identity without a graphic designer.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI can generate beautiful patterns, textures, and background designs for business cards. The approach: generate an abstract or branded background, then overlay your text and contact information in a design tool like Canva.</p>
</div>

<div class="prompt-box" style="background: var(--dim); border-radius: 8px; padding: 1.2rem 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; font-family: monospace; font-size: 1.05rem;">
  Abstract minimalist background for a business card, soft gradient from deep navy to warm coral, subtle geometric patterns, clean and professional, no text, high resolution
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Presentation Slides</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Never use a boring stock photo slide background again.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI-generated backgrounds transform presentations from forgettable to memorable. The key is creating images that are visually interesting but not so busy that they compete with your content.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Workflow:</strong> Generate a wide-format (16:9) image with soft focus or abstract styling. Use keywords like "soft bokeh background," "subtle gradient," or "blurred atmospheric scene." Import into PowerPoint, Google Slides, or Keynote as a slide background. Add a semi-transparent overlay if needed for text readability.</p>
  <p class="section-text">For a 10-slide presentation, generate backgrounds in a consistent style — same color palette, same artistic approach — so the whole deck feels cohesive. Use your brand prompt technique from Lesson 6.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">YouTube Thumbnails</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Thumbnails are the single biggest factor in whether someone clicks your video.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Great YouTube thumbnails are bold, high-contrast, and immediately readable. AI can generate the background scene, dramatic lighting effects, and eye-catching compositions. Then you add text and your face (if you appear on camera) in a tool like Canva or Photoshop.</p>
</div>

<div class="prompt-box" style="background: var(--dim); border-radius: 8px; padding: 1.2rem 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; font-family: monospace; font-size: 1.05rem;">
  Dramatic YouTube thumbnail background, bright explosion of orange and blue light, dark edges for contrast, space on the right side for text overlay, cinematic and energetic, 16:9 aspect ratio
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Product Mockups</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Visualize products before they exist.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Launching a t-shirt line? Creating a candle brand? Designing packaging? AI can generate realistic mockups that show your concept in context — your product on a shelf, in someone's hands, in a beautiful lifestyle setting. This is invaluable for testing ideas before investing in production.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Use specific, descriptive prompts. "A white ceramic candle in a minimalist glass jar with a kraft paper label, sitting on a marble bathroom shelf next to eucalyptus stems, soft natural light, product photography" gives you something you could literally put on an Etsy listing.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Blog Headers and Email Graphics</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Every piece of content you publish can now have a custom visual.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Blog posts with images get dramatically more engagement than text-only posts. AI makes it trivial to generate a custom header image for every article, newsletter, or email campaign. Match the image to the topic, keep your brand style consistent, and you have a professional content operation running on almost zero design budget.</p>
  <p class="section-text">For email graphics, keep images simple and bold — they need to look good at small sizes and load quickly. Flat illustrations, clean icons, and simple scenes work better than complex detailed images in email contexts.</p>
</div>
