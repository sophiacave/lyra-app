<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Big Picture</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">You now have all the skills. The final step is building a system.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Over nine lessons, you have gone from "what is AI image generation?" to understanding tools, writing advanced prompts, editing and refining, creating social content, generating video, building real projects, and navigating ethics. That is a massive amount of capability. Now we make it sustainable. A workflow means you do not have to think about process every time — you just create.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Your Prompt Library</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Save every prompt that works. This is your most valuable creative asset.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Create a document (Google Doc, Notion page, Apple Notes — whatever you actually use) with these sections:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Brand prompts:</strong> Your base style descriptions that keep your visual identity consistent. Save 2-3 variations for different moods (bright and energetic, calm and sophisticated, bold and dramatic).</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Proven winners:</strong> Every time you write a prompt that produces an outstanding result, save it here with a note about what made it work. Over time, this becomes a goldmine of tested language.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Style keywords:</strong> A running list of style, lighting, composition, and mood terms that you have found effective. Organized by category so you can mix and match.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Templates:</strong> Pre-built prompt structures for your most common needs — social media posts, blog headers, presentations, thumbnails. Fill-in-the-blank style so you can produce consistent results fast.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Your File Organization</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">A simple folder structure prevents chaos as your library grows.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Create a main "AI Visuals" folder with subfolders:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>By project:</strong> Website / Social Media / Presentations / Client Work / Personal<br>
  <strong>By status:</strong> Within each project folder, keep "Drafts" and "Finals" separate<br>
  <strong>By source:</strong> Optionally tag or note which tool generated each image — helpful when you need to go back and create variations</p>
  <p class="section-text">Delete ruthlessly. If you generated 10 images and picked 2 winners, delete the other 8. A curated library is infinitely more useful than a digital junk drawer.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Multi-Tool Pipeline</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The most powerful workflow combines several tools, each doing what it does best.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Here is an example pipeline that many creators use:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Step 1 — Concept:</strong> Use ChatGPT to brainstorm and refine your idea through conversation. Describe what you want and let the AI help you sharpen the concept before you even start generating images.<br>
  <strong>Step 2 — Generate:</strong> Create your base image in Midjourney (for beauty) or DALL-E (for precision) or Stable Diffusion (for control).<br>
  <strong>Step 3 — Refine:</strong> Bring the image into ChatGPT or Photoshop for inpainting and edits. Fix any details that are not right.<br>
  <strong>Step 4 — Enhance:</strong> Upscale with Topaz or Upscayl if needed. Adjust colors and contrast in Lightroom or a free tool like Photopea.<br>
  <strong>Step 5 — Design:</strong> Add text, branding, and layout in Canva, Figma, or your design tool of choice.<br>
  <strong>Step 6 — Animate (optional):</strong> Turn the final image into video with Runway or Pika for social media content.</p>
  <p class="section-text">You do not need every step for every project. Sometimes DALL-E gives you exactly what you need in one generation. But having the full pipeline means you can handle anything.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Weekly Creative Practice</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Fifteen minutes a week keeps your skills sharp and your library growing.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Set aside a short window each week to experiment. Try a new style you have never explored. Test a new tool feature. Generate images for fun, not just for work. This play time is where you make your biggest creative breakthroughs, because the pressure is off and curiosity takes over.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Follow AI art communities on social media. Look at what other people are creating and reverse-engineer their prompts. Share your own work and get feedback. The AI visual community is one of the most supportive creative spaces online right now.</p>
</div>
