<!-- SECTION 1 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Real Problem</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">You don't have a productivity problem. You have a clarity problem.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Most people don't struggle because they're lazy. They struggle because everything feels equally important and they can't figure out where to start. So they do the easy thing (scroll, snack, reorganize their desk) instead of the right thing.</p>
  <p class="section-text">AI is brilliant at this exact problem. Give it everything on your mind — the messy, unfiltered list — and ask it to help you sort, prioritize, and plan. It takes the chaos in your head and turns it into a clear path forward.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Brain Dump</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Step one: get it all out of your head.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.75rem">
    <p style="margin-bottom:1rem">The brain dump is the most powerful move in personal productivity. Here's how to do it with AI:</p>
    <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;margin-bottom:1rem">
      <p style="color:var(--orange);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.5rem">The prompt:</p>
      <code style="font-size:0.9rem">"Here's everything on my plate right now: [list everything — work tasks, personal errands, appointments, things you're worried about, stuff you keep forgetting]. Help me organize this into categories and tell me what to focus on first."</code>
    </div>
    <p style="font-size:0.9rem;color:var(--dim)">Don't filter. Don't organize. Just dump. Include "buy birthday card for mom" next to "finish quarterly report." AI doesn't care about the order — it'll sort it for you.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 3 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Matrix</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Let AI run the priority matrix for you.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <p style="margin-bottom:1rem">After your brain dump, ask AI to sort your tasks into four buckets:</p>
    <div style="display:grid;grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr;gap:0.75rem">
      <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;border-left:3px solid var(--red)">
        <p style="font-weight:600;font-size:0.9rem;color:var(--red)">Do Now</p>
        <p style="font-size:0.85rem;color:var(--dim)">Urgent + important. These are today's priorities.</p>
      </div>
      <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;border-left:3px solid var(--orange)">
        <p style="font-weight:600;font-size:0.9rem;color:var(--orange)">Schedule</p>
        <p style="font-size:0.85rem;color:var(--dim)">Important but not urgent. Block time this week.</p>
      </div>
      <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;border-left:3px solid var(--blue)">
        <p style="font-weight:600;font-size:0.9rem;color:var(--blue)">Quick Wins</p>
        <p style="font-size:0.85rem;color:var(--dim)">Under 5 minutes. Batch and knock them out.</p>
      </div>
      <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;border-left:3px solid var(--dim)">
        <p style="font-weight:600;font-size:0.9rem">Let Go</p>
        <p style="font-size:0.85rem;color:var(--dim)">Not important. Drop it or delegate it.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Break It Down</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Big projects become small steps.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The number one reason people procrastinate on big tasks: the task feels too large to start. "Clean the garage" sits on the list for months because it's actually 15 smaller tasks pretending to be one.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Ask AI: <em>"Break 'clean the garage' into steps I can do in 15-minute chunks."</em> Suddenly you have a list that starts with "grab a trash bag and throw away obvious junk from the left wall." That's doable. That's a start.</p>
  <p class="section-text">This works for everything: "plan mom's birthday party," "switch car insurance," "learn to cook." AI turns the mountain into a staircase.</p>
</div>

<!-- TRY IT -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Try It</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Do a brain dump right now.</h2>
  <div class="try-it-box">
    <p>Set a 3-minute timer. Write down everything on your mind — every task, errand, worry, and half-finished project. Then paste it into AI with this prompt:</p>
    <div class="prompt-box">
      <code>Here's my brain dump of everything I need to do: [PASTE LIST]. Sort these into four categories: Do Now (urgent + important), Schedule This Week (important, not urgent), Quick Wins (under 5 min), and Let Go (not worth my energy). Then give me my top 3 priorities for today.</code>
    </div>
    <p style="margin-top:1rem;color:var(--dim)">Bonus: pick your biggest, most procrastinated task from the list and ask AI to break it into 15-minute chunks.</p>
  </div>
</div>
