<!-- SECTION 1 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">A New Way to Learn</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">AI doesn't just give you answers. It teaches you.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Remember the best teacher you ever had? The one who could explain anything in a way that just clicked? AI can be that — available 24/7, endlessly patient, and never annoyed when you ask the same question three different ways.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The key difference between searching Google and asking AI: you can have a back-and-forth. You can say "I don't understand that part" or "can you give me a real-world example?" and the explanation adapts to you in real time.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Technique</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The "explain like I'm 10" method.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.75rem">
    <p style="margin-bottom:1rem">When you're learning something new, start simple and go deeper:</p>
    <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;margin-bottom:0.75rem">
      <p style="color:var(--green);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Level 1: The basics</p>
      <code style="font-size:0.9rem">"Explain how a 401(k) works like I'm 10 years old."</code>
    </div>
    <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;margin-bottom:0.75rem">
      <p style="color:var(--orange);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Level 2: Go deeper</p>
      <code style="font-size:0.9rem">"OK, now explain employer matching and vesting schedules. Use an example with real numbers."</code>
    </div>
    <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px">
      <p style="color:var(--purple);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Level 3: Apply it</p>
      <code style="font-size:0.9rem">"I make $55,000 and my employer matches 4%. How much should I contribute and why?"</code>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Trust but Verify</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">When to double-check AI's answers.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI is great at explaining concepts, breaking down ideas, and helping you think through decisions. It's less reliable for very specific facts — exact prices, current laws in your state, or whether a specific business is still open.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Rule of thumb:</strong> use AI to understand the landscape and know what questions to ask. Then verify specific facts (prices, deadlines, regulations) with official sources. AI gets you 80% there; you confirm the last 20%.</p>
</div>

<!-- TRY IT -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Try It</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Learn something you've been curious about.</h2>
  <div class="try-it-box">
    <p>Pick a topic you've been meaning to understand — something you've Googled before but never quite grasped. Use the three-level approach:</p>
    <div class="prompt-box">
      <code>Explain [TOPIC] to me like I have zero background in it. Start with the absolute basics, use a real-world analogy, and then tell me the three most important things I should know about it.</code>
    </div>
    <p style="margin-top:1rem;color:var(--dim)">After the first answer, go deeper: "Now explain [specific part] with a concrete example." Keep going until you feel like you actually get it. That's the magic of conversational learning.</p>
  </div>
