<!-- SECTION 1 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Mental Load</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">"What's for dinner?" is the most exhausting question in the world.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">It's not the cooking that drains people. It's the deciding. Every single day, you have to figure out what to eat, whether you have the ingredients, whether it fits your budget, and whether anyone else in the house will eat it. That decision fatigue adds up fast.</p>
  <p class="section-text">AI takes the deciding off your plate (pun intended). Give it your constraints once — dietary needs, budget, skill level, who you're feeding — and it generates a plan. You just shop and cook.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Meal Planning</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">A full week of meals in 60 seconds.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.75rem">
    <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;margin-bottom:1rem">
      <p style="color:var(--orange);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.5rem">The one-prompt meal plan:</p>
      <code style="font-size:0.9rem;line-height:1.6">"Make me a 5-day dinner plan. Budget: about $60 total. I cook for 2 people. We don't eat seafood. I have about 30 minutes on weeknights. Include a grocery list organized by store section."</code>
    </div>
    <p style="font-size:0.9rem;color:var(--dim)">AI gives you five dinners with recipes, a combined grocery list, and often suggests ways to reuse ingredients across meals (cook extra chicken Monday, use it in Wednesday's salad).</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 3 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Fridge Audit</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Use what you already have. Waste nothing.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <p style="margin-bottom:1rem">Before you go shopping, try this:</p>
    <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;margin-bottom:1rem">
      <code style="font-size:0.9rem">"I have these ingredients that need to be used soon: half a bag of spinach, 4 eggs, some feta cheese, a can of chickpeas, and a lemon. What can I make for dinner tonight?"</code>
    </div>
    <p style="font-size:0.9rem">AI will suggest multiple options — a spinach and feta frittata, a chickpea and spinach bowl, lemon-herb egg cups. You save money and reduce food waste. This prompt alone is worth the entire course.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Beyond Food</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Fitness, wellness, and knowing AI's limits.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="margin-bottom:1rem">
      <p style="color:var(--green);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">What AI can do well</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">Create beginner workout plans, suggest stretching routines, explain nutrition basics, help you track habits, build a sleep hygiene checklist, and brainstorm ways to move more during your day.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="margin-bottom:1rem">
      <p style="color:var(--purple);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Great prompts to try</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">"I sit at a desk all day and my back hurts. Give me a 10-minute stretching routine I can do at my desk."</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem;margin-top:0.5rem">"I want to start walking more. Build me a 4-week plan that starts easy and works up to 10,000 steps."</p>
    </div>
    <div style="background:var(--bg);padding:1rem;border-radius:8px;border-left:3px solid var(--red)">
      <p style="color:var(--red);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Important boundary</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">AI is not a doctor, dietitian, or therapist. It can help you plan and think through options, but for medical advice, diagnoses, or treatment plans — always consult a real professional. Use AI to prepare good questions for your doctor, not to replace them.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- TRY IT -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Try It</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Plan your meals or raid your fridge.</h2>
  <div class="try-it-box">
    <p>Choose one of these two exercises:</p>
    <div class="prompt-box">
      <code>Option A: "Make me a 3-day dinner plan for [number] people. Budget: $[amount]. Dietary needs: [any]. Cooking time: [minutes] per meal. Include a grocery list."</code>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-box" style="margin-top:0.75rem">
      <code>Option B: "I have these ingredients: [list what's in your fridge]. What are 3 meals I can make tonight without buying anything else?"</code>
    </div>
    <p style="margin-top:1rem;color:var(--dim)">If you try the meal plan, ask a follow-up: "Can you make Monday's recipe easier? I'll be tired." Watch how AI adjusts.</p>
  </div>
