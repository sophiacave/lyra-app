<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Frameworks Review</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The productivity frameworks that power your system.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="margin-bottom:1.25rem">
      <p style="color:var(--orange);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">1. The Context Dump (Lesson 2)</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.95rem">Share your real situation — constraints, frustrations, goals — and let AI build a plan around your reality. Works for morning routines, weekly planning, and any situation where generic advice fails. The more honest you are, the better the plan.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="margin-bottom:1.25rem">
      <p style="color:var(--purple);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">2. Tone + Context + Goal (Lesson 3)</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.95rem">The universal framework for any written communication. Tell AI how it should sound, who you're talking to and why, and what you want to happen. This framework works for emails, messages, letters, complaints, and negotiations.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="margin-bottom:1.25rem">
      <p style="color:var(--green);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">3. The Brain Dump + Priority Matrix (Lesson 4)</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.95rem">Get everything out of your head. Every task, errand, worry, half-finished project. Then let AI sort it into four buckets: Do Now, Schedule, Quick Wins, and Let Go. This solves the clarity problem that masquerades as a productivity problem.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="margin-bottom:1.25rem">
      <p style="color:var(--blue);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">4. The Three-Level Learning Method (Lesson 5)</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.95rem">Level 1: Explain it like I'm 10. Level 2: Go deeper with real examples. Level 3: Apply it to my specific situation. This progressive approach builds genuine understanding, not surface familiarity.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="margin-bottom:1.25rem">
      <p style="color:var(--red);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">5. The Constraint-First Method (Lessons 6-8)</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.95rem">Whether planning meals, managing money, or booking travel — give AI your constraints first (budget, time, dietary needs, group size). Constraints make plans realistic. Without them, you get generic aspirational advice you'll never follow.</p>
    </div>
    <div>
      <p style="color:var(--orange);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">6. The Starter Spark (Lesson 9)</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.95rem">Beat the blank page by asking AI for 5 options — opening lines, project ideas, chord progressions. Pick the one that resonates and run with it. AI gives you the launch; you do the flying.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

<!-- COMMON MISTAKES -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Common Mistakes</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Pitfalls to avoid as you build your AI productivity habit.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="margin-bottom:1rem;padding-bottom:1rem;border-bottom:1px solid var(--dim)">
      <p style="color:var(--red);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Being too vague</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">Saying "help me be more productive" gets you generic advice. Saying "here are my 12 tasks for today, I have 4 free hours, prioritize them" gets you a real plan. Specificity is everything.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="margin-bottom:1rem;padding-bottom:1rem;border-bottom:1px solid var(--dim)">
      <p style="color:var(--red);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Trying to do everything at once</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">Master one workflow before adding the next. The daily check-in is a great place to start. Once that's a habit, add email drafting. Then meal planning. Build the system in layers.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="margin-bottom:1rem;padding-bottom:1rem;border-bottom:1px solid var(--dim)">
      <p style="color:var(--red);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Trusting AI output without review</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">AI is a first draft machine. Always read the output. Check facts. Verify recipes make sense. Read emails out loud before sending. Your judgment is the final quality gate.</p>
    </div>
    <div>
      <p style="color:var(--red);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Giving up after one bad result</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">If AI's first answer isn't great, say "try a different approach" or give more context. The first prompt is rarely the best. Iteration is how you get great results.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

<!-- WHAT MAKES THIS STICK -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Making It Stick</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The habits that separate people who use AI from people who tried it once.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Knowledge without practice fades. The people who get lasting value from AI are the ones who build small, consistent habits. Here's what the research on habit formation tells us about making your AI productivity system permanent.</p>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="margin-bottom:1rem">
      <p style="color:var(--green);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Habit stacking</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">Attach your AI check-in to something you already do every morning — making coffee, sitting at your desk, opening your laptop. "After I pour my coffee, I do my 5-minute AI check-in." Existing habits anchor new ones.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="margin-bottom:1rem">
      <p style="color:var(--purple);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Start absurdly small</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">Don't commit to using AI for everything. Commit to one prompt per day. After a week, it's automatic. After a month, you'll naturally start using AI for more things because the habit is already in place.</p>
    </div>
    <div>
      <p style="color:var(--orange);font-weight:600;margin-bottom:0.25rem">Track your wins</p>
      <p style="font-size:0.9rem">Keep a simple note of times AI saved you real time or mental energy. "AI drafted that complaint email in 2 minutes instead of 20." Seeing the evidence makes the habit feel valuable, which makes it stick.</p>
    </div>
