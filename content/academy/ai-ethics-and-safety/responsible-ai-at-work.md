<!-- SECTION 1 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Reality</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Most companies don't have an AI policy. Yet.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Studies show that the majority of employees are already using AI at work — but most companies haven't established formal policies around it. This creates a gray zone where well-meaning people can accidentally violate client confidentiality, create legal liability, or undermine trust.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Whether your company has a policy or not, <strong>you need your own framework for responsible AI use at work.</strong></p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Checklist</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">5 questions before using AI for any work task.</h2>

<!-- SECTION 4 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">No Policy Yet?</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">What to do when your company has no AI policy.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Apply the "front page test": would you be comfortable if your AI use appeared on the front page of your industry's top publication? If yes, proceed. If you hesitate, reconsider.</p>
  <p class="section-text">And when in doubt: <strong>be more cautious with other people's data than your own.</strong> Your career can survive being called old-fashioned. It may not survive a data breach.</p>
</div>
