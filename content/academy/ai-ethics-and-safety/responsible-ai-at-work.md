<!-- SECTION 1 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Reality</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Most companies don't have an AI policy. Yet.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Studies show that the majority of employees are already using AI at work — but most companies haven't established formal policies around it. This creates a gray zone where well-meaning people can accidentally violate client confidentiality, create legal liability, or undermine trust.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Whether your company has a policy or not, <strong>you need your own framework for responsible AI use at work.</strong></p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">For Teams</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Bringing AI into your team responsibly.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">If you're a manager or team lead, here's how to introduce AI without chaos:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(74,222,128,.12);color:var(--green);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">✓</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Start with guidelines, not bans.</strong> Blanket bans just drive AI use underground. Clear guidelines make it safe and visible.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(74,222,128,.12);color:var(--green);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">✓</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Define approved tools.</strong> Which AI tools can your team use? Free accounts? Paid? Company-licensed?</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(74,222,128,.12);color:var(--green);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">✓</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Set data boundaries.</strong> What can and can't go into AI tools? Make this extremely specific.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(74,222,128,.12);color:var(--green);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">✓</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Require human review.</strong> All AI output that goes to clients or gets published should be reviewed by a person.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(74,222,128,.12);color:var(--green);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">✓</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Train, don't just regulate.</strong> Teach your team HOW to use AI well. Rules without skills lead to frustration.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

<!-- SECTION 4 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">No Policy Yet?</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">What to do when your company has no AI policy.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Apply the "front page test": would you be comfortable if your AI use appeared on the front page of your industry's top publication? If yes, proceed. If you hesitate, reconsider.</p>
  <p class="section-text">And when in doubt: <strong>be more cautious with other people's data than your own.</strong> Your career can survive being called old-fashioned. It may not survive a data breach.</p>
</div>
