<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Landscape</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The law is evolving fast. Here's where it stands.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">As of now, pure AI-generated content — with no meaningful human creative input — generally <strong>cannot be copyrighted</strong> in the United States. The U.S. Copyright Office has consistently held that copyright requires human authorship.</p>
  <p class="section-text">However, content that is <strong>substantially created by a human with AI assistance</strong> — where the human made creative decisions about selection, arrangement, and editing — can likely be copyrighted. The human creativity is what gets protection.</p>
  <p class="section-text">This area is developing rapidly with ongoing court cases and new regulations. What's true today may change. But the principle is clear: <strong>the more human creativity in the process, the stronger your IP position.</strong></p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Others' IP</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">How to avoid infringing others' intellectual property.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI can sometimes produce output that closely resembles existing copyrighted works — especially when prompted with specific references. Here's how to stay safe:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">1</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Don't ask AI to reproduce copyrighted text.</strong> "Rewrite this New York Times article" or "write something exactly like [author's name]" crosses lines.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">2</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Be cautious with "in the style of."</strong> Asking for general style inspiration is usually fine. Asking for a clone of someone's distinctive voice is riskier.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">3</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Don't feed others' proprietary content into AI.</strong> Pasting a competitor's strategy deck into AI to "analyze" it may violate their IP rights.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">4</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Check AI image generation terms.</strong> Different AI image tools have different commercial use policies. Read the terms before using images commercially.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">5</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>When in doubt, add your own creativity.</strong> The more you transform, edit, and add to AI output, the stronger your position — both legally and ethically.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

<!-- SECTION 4 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Practical Guidelines</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">For commercial use of AI-generated content.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">If you're using AI content in a business context:</p>
  <ul class="section-text" style="padding-left:1.5rem;margin-top:.5rem;margin-bottom:1rem">
    <li><strong>Always add meaningful human editing.</strong> Don't publish raw AI output as your own work.</li>
    <li><strong>Keep records of your creative process.</strong> If IP ownership is ever questioned, showing your prompts, edits, and creative direction strengthens your position.</li>
    <li><strong>Review your AI tool's terms of service.</strong> Most major providers grant you ownership of outputs, but the specifics vary.</li>
    <li><strong>Stay current.</strong> This area of law is changing rapidly. What's gray today may be black or white tomorrow.</li>
  </ul>
</div>
