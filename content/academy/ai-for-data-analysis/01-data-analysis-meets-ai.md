<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--orange);">The Old Way</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Data Analysis Before AI</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Think about the last time you stared at a spreadsheet full of numbers. Maybe it was sales figures, survey responses, or your personal budget. You probably spent hours sorting columns, writing formulas, and trying to spot patterns with your own eyes.</p>
  <p class="section-text">That approach works — but it's exhausting. You need to know Excel formulas, understand pivot tables, maybe even write SQL or Python. The barrier to entry is high, and the time investment is brutal.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Most people give up before they find the insight that actually matters.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--purple);">The Shift</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">What AI Actually Changes</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI doesn't just speed up the old process. It fundamentally changes the relationship between you and your data. Instead of learning tools, you describe what you want to know. Instead of building formulas, you ask questions in plain English.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Here's the shift: <strong>you become the thinker, AI becomes the calculator.</strong> Your job is to ask the right questions. AI's job is to crunch the numbers, spot the patterns, and present the answers.</p>
  <p class="section-text">This means anyone — regardless of technical background — can do serious data analysis. A small business owner can analyze their sales trends. A teacher can find patterns in student performance. A nonprofit can measure their impact.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--green);">Real Example</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">60-Second Analysis</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Imagine you have a CSV file with 12 months of sales data. In the old world, you'd open Excel, create charts, write SUMIF formulas, build pivot tables. That's an afternoon.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="border-left: 3px solid var(--green); padding: 1rem; background: var(--bg);">
    <p><strong>With AI, you paste the data and say:</strong></p>
    <p><em>"Analyze this sales data. What are the top 3 trends? Which month was strongest and why? Are there any concerning patterns?"</em></p>
    <p style="color: var(--dim);">In seconds, you get a structured breakdown with specific numbers, percentage changes, and actionable insights. No formulas. No pivot tables. Just answers.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--blue);">Key Concept</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The Analysis Mindset</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The most important skill in AI-powered data analysis isn't technical — it's <strong>curiosity</strong>. The better your questions, the better your insights. Throughout this course, we'll train that muscle.</p>
  <p class="section-text">You'll learn to think like a data analyst without needing to code like one. That's the promise of AI for data analysis, and it's real.</p>
</div>

<div class="try-it-box">
  <h3>Try It Yourself</h3>
  <p>Take any small dataset you have — a bank statement, a list of expenses, even your Spotify listening history. Paste it into Claude and try this prompt:</p>
  <div class="prompt-box"><code>Here's my [type of data]. Give me a summary of the key patterns, the most surprising finding, and one actionable recommendation.</code></div>
  <p style="color: var(--dim);">Notice how natural it feels. No technical setup. Just a conversation with your data.</p>
</div>
