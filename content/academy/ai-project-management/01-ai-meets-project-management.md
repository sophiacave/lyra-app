<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--orange);">The Shift</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Project Management Was Waiting for This</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Project managers spend roughly 60% of their time on coordination — status updates, meeting notes, report formatting, chasing action items. That's not strategy. That's overhead.</p>
  <p class="section-text">AI doesn't replace your judgment about what matters. It takes on the repetitive work so you can focus on the decisions that actually move projects forward. Think of it as having an infinitely patient assistant who never forgets a detail.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--purple);">The Big Three</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Where AI Hits Hardest in PM</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>1. Information Processing.</strong> AI can digest a 90-minute meeting transcript and pull out every action item, decision, and open question in seconds. No more re-watching recordings at 2x speed.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>2. Communication Generation.</strong> Status reports, stakeholder updates, executive summaries — AI drafts them from your raw notes. You review and send. What took 45 minutes now takes 5.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>3. Pattern Recognition.</strong> AI spots risks you might miss. It notices when timelines are slipping, when dependencies are stacking up, when team workload is unbalanced. It doesn't get tired or distracted.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--green);">Real Talk</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">What AI Won't Do</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI won't navigate office politics. It won't sense that your lead developer is burning out from body language in a standup. It won't know that the client's "this is fine" email actually means "I hate this."</p>
  <p class="section-text">The human side of project management — empathy, relationship building, reading the room — that's still yours. And honestly, that's the part that matters most. AI just clears the path so you have energy left for it.</p>
</div>

<div class="demo-container">
  <h3>See It In Action</h3>
  <p>Here's what a simple AI-assisted PM workflow looks like:</p>
  <ol>
    <li><strong>Before the meeting:</strong> AI generates an agenda from last week's action items</li>
    <li><strong>During the meeting:</strong> AI transcribes and captures key points</li>
    <li><strong>After the meeting:</strong> AI produces a summary, action items, and a status update for stakeholders</li>
    <li><strong>Between meetings:</strong> AI tracks progress against the plan and flags risks</li>
  </ol>
  <p>That entire loop used to take hours of manual work. With AI, it's mostly automatic.</p>
</div>

<div class="try-it-box">
  <h3>Try It Yourself</h3>
  <p>Take a recent project update you wrote manually. Paste your raw notes into Claude and try this prompt:</p>
  <div class="prompt-box"><code>I have raw notes from a project check-in. Please extract: (1) decisions made, (2) action items with owners, (3) open questions, and (4) risks or blockers. Then draft a 3-paragraph status update for stakeholders. Here are my notes: [paste notes]</code></div>
  <p>Compare the AI output with what you would have written. Notice what it catches that you might have missed.</p>
</div>
