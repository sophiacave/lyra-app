<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--orange);">The System</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Tools Are Nothing Without a System</h2>
  <p class="section-text">You now have prompts for planning, meetings, reporting, risk, resources, documentation, agile, and stakeholder communication. That's a lot of individual tools. The difference between someone who uses AI occasionally and someone who runs their entire PM practice with AI is a system — a repeatable workflow that ties everything together.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Your system doesn't need to be complex. It needs to be consistent. Here's how to build one that sticks.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--purple);">The Weekly Rhythm</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Monday to Friday with AI</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Monday:</strong> Review last week's action items with AI. Generate this week's priorities. Draft the team update.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Daily:</strong> Process meeting notes through AI immediately after each meeting. Action items go to your tracker within minutes, not days.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Wednesday:</strong> Mid-week risk check. Feed AI your current status and risk register. Adjust anything that's shifted.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Friday:</strong> Status report generation. Dump raw data, get polished outputs for every audience. Update resource allocations for the following week.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Total AI time per week: about 2 hours. Time saved: 6-8 hours. That math works every single week.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--green);">Making It Stick</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The First 30 Days</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Week 1:</strong> Pick one workflow — meeting notes is the easiest win. Use AI for every meeting this week. Get comfortable with the rhythm.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Week 2:</strong> Add status reporting. Build your Friday reporting habit. Send the AI-drafted updates and see how people respond.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Week 3:</strong> Layer in risk assessment and resource tracking. Your project visibility just tripled.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Week 4:</strong> Go full system. All templates active. You're now running a modern PM practice that gives you hours back every week for the work that actually matters — leading your team, solving problems, making decisions.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--red);">Common Mistakes</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">What to Watch Out For</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Don't over-automate.</strong> AI drafts, you decide. Every output gets your review before it goes to anyone. Your judgment is the quality gate.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Don't hide the AI.</strong> Telling your team "I use AI to process meeting notes" builds trust. Pretending you hand-crafted a 10-page project charter over the weekend does not.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Don't skip the context.</strong> AI outputs are only as good as the context you provide. Spend the extra 30 seconds adding team constraints, stakeholder preferences, and project history. It pays back tenfold.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--orange);">Master Template</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The Universal PM Prompt Structure</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Every prompt in this course follows the same underlying structure. Master this pattern and you can create effective prompts for any PM task, even ones we did not cover:</p>
  <div class="prompt-box"><code>You are an experienced [role relevant to the task — project manager, scrum master, program director].

CONTEXT:
- Project: [name, type, current phase]
- Team: [size, composition, constraints]
- Timeline: [where we are, what is coming]
- Stakeholders: [who cares about this output]
- History: [relevant past decisions, changes, patterns]

TASK:
[Clearly state what you need — be specific about deliverables]

FORMAT:
- [Specify structure — headers, bullets, tables, narrative]
- [Specify length — one page, three paragraphs, 200 words]
- [Specify tone — formal, conversational, urgent]

CONSTRAINTS:
- [What to include]
- [What to exclude]
- [Assumptions to flag]

Please also identify anything I may have overlooked or any assumptions in my request that could lead to problems.</code></div>
  <p class="section-text">This six-part structure — Role, Context, Task, Format, Constraints, and Self-Check — produces consistently high-quality output. The self-check at the end is the secret weapon: it turns AI into a thinking partner, not just a text generator.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--purple);">Framework</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Measuring Your AI PM Impact</h2>
  <p class="section-text">You cannot improve what you do not measure. Track these metrics over your first 90 days of AI-assisted PM to quantify the impact:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Time Metrics:</strong></p>
  <p class="section-text">Hours spent on reporting before vs. after AI adoption. Hours spent on meeting processing before vs. after. Hours spent on planning and documentation before vs. after. Most PMs see a 40-60% reduction in these categories within the first month.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Quality Metrics:</strong></p>
  <p class="section-text">Number of action items that slip through the cracks (should decrease). Stakeholder satisfaction with reporting clarity (ask for feedback). Frequency of scope misunderstandings (should decrease as documentation improves). Risk identification rate — how many risks were caught proactively vs. discovered as surprises.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Capacity Metrics:</strong></p>
  <p class="section-text">Hours per week you spend on strategic work vs. administrative work (track for two weeks before AI, then track again after one month). Number of proactive communications sent vs. reactive responses. Time spent coaching team members vs. chasing status updates.</p>
  <p class="section-text">These numbers tell a compelling story when your manager asks "what is AI doing for you?" — and they tell you where to focus your next round of optimization.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--green);">Real-World Example</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The 90-Day Transformation</h2>
  <p class="section-text">A PM at a healthcare company tracked his workflow for two weeks before adopting AI-assisted practices, then measured again at 30, 60, and 90 days:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Day 0 (baseline):</strong> 12 hours/week on reporting and status updates. 8 hours/week on meeting processing. 4 hours/week on documentation. Strategic work: about 6 hours/week. Constantly felt behind.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Day 30:</strong> Reporting dropped to 4 hours/week (meeting notes template adopted, status report template working). Documentation unchanged. Strategic work: 10 hours/week. Starting to feel in control.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Day 60:</strong> Reporting at 3 hours/week (process refined). Meeting processing at 3 hours/week (all templates dialed in). Documentation at 2 hours/week (SOPs being generated from tribal knowledge). Strategic work: 14 hours/week. Proactively identifying risks before they became problems.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Day 90:</strong> Total administrative PM work: 8 hours/week (down from 24). Strategic work: 20 hours/week. His project hit every milestone on time for the first time in two years. His stakeholders rated his communication clarity at 4.7/5, up from 3.2. He received a promotion at his next review cycle.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The AI did not make him smarter. It made him faster at the work that was stealing his time — and that gave him the space to do the work that actually differentiated him as a PM.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--red);">Advanced</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Building Your AI PM Knowledge Base</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The most powerful AI PM technique is one that compounds over time: building a project knowledge base that makes AI smarter about YOUR specific context.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>What to save:</strong> Every project charter, risk register, retro summary, lessons learned, and stakeholder map you create. These are not just records — they are training data for future AI conversations.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>How to use it:</strong> When starting a new project, feed AI your previous project's lessons learned: "Here is what went wrong on our last three projects. What patterns do you see, and how should I adjust my plan for this new project to avoid repeating them?" AI identifies systemic issues you might not see across individual projects.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>The compound effect:</strong> After six months of saving AI-assisted outputs, you have a personalized project management playbook. New team members can read it to understand how your organization runs projects. AI can reference it to give increasingly context-aware recommendations. Your institutional knowledge is no longer trapped in your head — it is documented, searchable, and growing.</p>
  <p class="section-text">This is the difference between using AI as a tool and building an AI-powered practice. Tools help you do tasks. A practice transforms how you work.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--blue);">The Future</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Where AI PM Is Heading</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The techniques in this course represent the current state of AI-assisted project management. But the field is evolving rapidly. Here is where it is heading:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Integrated AI in PM tools.</strong> Jira, Asana, Monday, and Linear are all building AI features directly into their platforms. Soon, your project management tool will draft status reports, flag risks, and suggest resource adjustments without you needing to copy-paste into a separate AI. The prompt skills you learned here will transfer directly to these integrated tools.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Predictive project intelligence.</strong> As AI gains access to more project data across organizations, it will move from reactive analysis to predictive intelligence. "Projects with this risk profile and team structure historically deliver 2-3 weeks late" — that kind of pattern matching will transform how we plan and estimate.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Autonomous PM agents.</strong> AI agents that monitor your project continuously — watching Slack for risk signals, tracking Jira for velocity changes, and proactively drafting updates when metrics shift. The PM becomes a strategic decision-maker supported by an always-on AI assistant that handles the monitoring and reporting automatically.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The PMs who are building prompt skills and AI workflows today will be the ones who lead these transitions tomorrow. You are not just learning a tool — you are building the foundation for the next decade of your career.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--orange);">Workflow</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Teaching AI About Your Organization</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI becomes dramatically more useful when it understands your specific organizational context. At the start of each major project, give AI a context brief:</p>
  <div class="prompt-box"><code>Here is context about my organization and team that should inform all of your responses in this conversation:

Organization: [company name, industry, size, culture — formal vs. startup]
My role: [your title and what you are responsible for]
Team: [size, composition, working style, tools they use]
Stakeholders: [key people, their roles, what they care about]
Communication norms: [formal emails vs. Slack, meeting-heavy vs. async, detail-loving vs. headline-only]
Current projects: [brief list of active work]
Known constraints: [budget limitations, hiring freezes, technical debt, regulatory requirements]

Please keep this context in mind for all subsequent requests in this conversation.</code></div>
  <p class="section-text">Setting this context once means every subsequent prompt in the conversation produces output that is tailored to your actual situation — not generic advice. The AI knows your CTO prefers bullet points, your client likes narrative, and your team runs two-week sprints. Every output reflects that without you having to repeat it.</p>
</div>
