<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--orange);">The System</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Tools Are Nothing Without a System</h2>
  <p class="section-text">You now have prompts for planning, meetings, reporting, risk, resources, documentation, agile, and stakeholder communication. That's a lot of individual tools. The difference between someone who uses AI occasionally and someone who runs their entire PM practice with AI is a system — a repeatable workflow that ties everything together.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Your system doesn't need to be complex. It needs to be consistent. Here's how to build one that sticks.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--purple);">The Weekly Rhythm</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Monday to Friday with AI</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Monday:</strong> Review last week's action items with AI. Generate this week's priorities. Draft the team update.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Daily:</strong> Process meeting notes through AI immediately after each meeting. Action items go to your tracker within minutes, not days.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Wednesday:</strong> Mid-week risk check. Feed AI your current status and risk register. Adjust anything that's shifted.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Friday:</strong> Status report generation. Dump raw data, get polished outputs for every audience. Update resource allocations for the following week.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Total AI time per week: about 2 hours. Time saved: 6-8 hours. That math works every single week.</p>
</div>

<div class="demo-container">
  <h3>Your Prompt Library</h3>
  <p>Save these as your core templates — adapt them to your projects:</p>
  <ol>
    <li><strong>Project Kickoff:</strong> WBS generation + risk register + resource plan</li>
    <li><strong>Meeting Processing:</strong> Summary + action items + next agenda</li>
    <li><strong>Weekly Status:</strong> Multi-audience report from raw data</li>
    <li><strong>Risk Review:</strong> Register update + new risk identification</li>
    <li><strong>Sprint Planning:</strong> Story writing + capacity check + sprint goal</li>
    <li><strong>Retro Analysis:</strong> Theme identification + action tracking</li>
    <li><strong>Stakeholder Update:</strong> Audience-tailored messaging</li>
    <li><strong>Doc Generation:</strong> SOP + process documentation from tribal knowledge</li>
  </ol>
  <p>Eight templates. That covers 90% of your PM communication needs.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--green);">Making It Stick</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The First 30 Days</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Week 1:</strong> Pick one workflow — meeting notes is the easiest win. Use AI for every meeting this week. Get comfortable with the rhythm.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Week 2:</strong> Add status reporting. Build your Friday reporting habit. Send the AI-drafted updates and see how people respond.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Week 3:</strong> Layer in risk assessment and resource tracking. Your project visibility just tripled.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Week 4:</strong> Go full system. All templates active. You're now running a modern PM practice that gives you hours back every week for the work that actually matters — leading your team, solving problems, making decisions.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--red);">Common Mistakes</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">What to Watch Out For</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Don't over-automate.</strong> AI drafts, you decide. Every output gets your review before it goes to anyone. Your judgment is the quality gate.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Don't hide the AI.</strong> Telling your team "I use AI to process meeting notes" builds trust. Pretending you hand-crafted a 10-page project charter over the weekend does not.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Don't skip the context.</strong> AI outputs are only as good as the context you provide. Spend the extra 30 seconds adding team constraints, stakeholder preferences, and project history. It pays back tenfold.</p>
</div>
