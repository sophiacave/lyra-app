<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--orange);">Why Email Still Wins</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The Channel You Own</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Social media algorithms change. Search rankings shift. But your email list? That's yours. No algorithm sits between you and your subscriber. When someone gives you their email, they're saying: "I trust you enough to let you into my inbox." That's sacred ground.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The problem is most email sequences are boring. They read like press releases or, worse, like AI slop. With the right approach, AI helps you write emails that feel like they came from a friend who happens to be an expert.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--purple);">The Welcome Sequence</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">First Impressions at Scale</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Your welcome sequence is the most important email series you'll ever write. It sets the tone for the entire relationship. Here's a 5-email structure that works:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Email 1 (Immediate):</strong> Deliver what you promised. Welcome them. Set expectations for what's coming.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Email 2 (Day 2):</strong> Share your story. Why do you do what you do? This builds connection.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Email 3 (Day 4):</strong> Deliver massive value. Your best tip, your most useful resource, your signature framework.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Email 4 (Day 6):</strong> Social proof and transformation. Show what's possible. Share a result, a testimonial, a case study.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Email 5 (Day 8):</strong> The gentle ask. Invite them to take the next step — buy, book, join, subscribe to the paid tier.</p>

  <div class="demo-container">
    <p><strong>Prompt for a welcome sequence:</strong></p>
    <p>"Write a 5-email welcome sequence for [business]. Subscriber signed up for [lead magnet]. My brand voice is [describe]. Email 1: deliver + welcome. Email 2: origin story. Email 3: best tip. Email 4: social proof. Email 5: soft pitch for [offer]. Keep each email under 300 words. Conversational, not salesy."</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--green);">The Story-Value-Ask Framework</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Every Email, Same Skeleton</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Whether it's a newsletter issue or a sales email, the best emails follow a simple pattern: open with a story or hook that creates connection, deliver genuine value that makes the reader glad they opened it, then close with a clear ask — even if the ask is just "hit reply and tell me what you think."</p>
  <p class="section-text">AI is excellent at generating story angles and value sections. Your job is to feed it real stories from your life and business. AI can shape them; only you can supply them.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label" style="color: var(--red);">Subject Lines</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The Gate to Everything</h2>
  <p class="section-text">A brilliant email with a boring subject line is a tree falling in an empty forest. Nobody hears it. Use AI to generate 20+ subject line options for every email, then pick the one that makes you most curious.</p>

  <div class="try-it-box">
    <div class="prompt-box"><code>Write 20 subject line options for an email about [topic]. Mix these styles: curiosity gap, specific number, personal/confessional, question, and urgency. Keep each under 50 characters. My audience is [describe] and my tone is [describe].</code></div>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text">Test two subject lines per send if your email platform supports A/B testing. Over time, you'll develop an instinct for what your audience opens — and AI will learn from your feedback too.</p>
</div>
