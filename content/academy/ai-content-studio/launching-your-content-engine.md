<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Engine</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Your content engine is a system, not a habit.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Habits break. Systems don't. A content engine is a repeatable process with defined inputs, clear steps, and predictable outputs. You've built every component — strategy, long-form, short-form, email, repurposing, brand voice, SEO, and batching. Now we wire them together.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The goal: produce consistent, high-quality content every week with less effort than you spent on a single blog post before this course.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Weekly Rhythm</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">A content week that actually works.</h2>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.75rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:16px">

        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Review last week's metrics. Pick topics from your calendar. Brief AI with context.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="background:var(--bg);border:1px solid rgba(251,146,60,.15);border-radius:8px;padding:12px 16px">

        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Draft 1-2 blog posts with AI. SEO-optimize as you go.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="background:var(--bg);border:1px solid rgba(74,222,128,.15);border-radius:8px;padding:12px 16px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;margin-bottom:4px"><span style="color:var(--green)">Wednesday</span> — Short-Form & Social (1 hr)</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Repurpose long-form into 5-7 social posts. Write newsletter draft.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="background:var(--bg);border:1px solid rgba(56,189,248,.15);border-radius:8px;padding:12px 16px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;margin-bottom:4px"><span style="color:var(--blue)">Thursday</span> — Review & Schedule (1 hr)</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Edit everything. Brand voice check. Schedule all posts. Queue emails.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="background:var(--bg);border:1px solid rgba(248,113,113,.15);border-radius:8px;padding:12px 16px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;margin-bottom:4px"><span style="color:var(--red)">Friday</span> — Engage & Analyze (30 min)</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Respond to comments. Check metrics. Note what's resonating.</div>
      </div>
    </div>

  <p class="section-text" style="margin-top:16px">Total: ~5.5 hours per week for a full content operation. Less time than most people spend on one blog post without AI.</p>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Measure</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Track what matters. Ignore what doesn't.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Three metrics: <strong>output consistency</strong> (hitting weekly targets?), <strong>engagement rate</strong> (people interacting?), and <strong>conversion rate</strong> (readers becoming customers?). Everything else is vanity.</p>
</div>
