  <h1>Long-Form <span class="accent">Writing.</span></h1>
  <p class="sub">Blog posts, articles, and guides that people actually finish reading.</p>

<div class="learn-card">

    <li>The outline-first method that prevents AI-generated mush</li>
    <li>How to co-write with AI without losing your voice</li>

    <li>The editing workflow that turns decent drafts into great ones</li>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Truth</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">AI can't write your blog post. But it can make you 5x faster.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Let's get this out of the way: if you prompt AI with "write me a 1500-word blog post about productivity," you'll get something generic, bland, and indistinguishable from a million other posts. That's not the move.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The move is using AI as a collaborator at each stage of the writing process — outlining, drafting, expanding, editing — while you bring the ideas, the stories, and the perspective that only you have.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Step 1</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Start with a conversation, not a command.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Before you write anything, talk to AI about your topic. Treat it like a brainstorm session with a smart colleague:</p>
  <p class="section-text"><em>"I want to write about [topic]. My take is [your unique angle]. My audience is [who]. Help me think through this: What are the common misconceptions about this topic? What questions would my readers have? What's the most surprising thing I could tell them?"</em></p>
  <p class="section-text">This conversation surfaces ideas you wouldn't find alone. It pressure-tests your angle. It reveals gaps in your thinking. Do this for 5 minutes before outlining and your final piece will be significantly stronger.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Step 2</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The outline is everything.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">A strong outline makes writing almost effortless. A weak outline (or no outline) leads to rambling, repetition, and that dreaded feeling of being "stuck in the middle."</p>
  <p class="section-text"><em>"Create an outline for a [length] blog post about [topic]. My angle is [describe]. Structure it with: a hook that makes the reader feel [emotion], 3-4 main sections with clear takeaways, and a closing that drives [action]. For each section, include the key point and one specific example or data point I should include."</em></p>
  <p class="section-text">Review the outline before you write. Move sections around. Cut what doesn't serve the reader. Add your personal stories where they fit. The 10 minutes you spend on the outline saves an hour of rewriting later.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Step 3</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Draft in layers, not all at once.</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong style="color:var(--orange)">Layer 1 — The rough draft:</strong> Write fast. Don't edit. Get your ideas down in your own words, even if they're messy. This is where your voice lives. AI can't replicate your lived experience, your weird metaphors, your authentic perspective. Put those in first.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong style="color:var(--purple)">Layer 2 — AI expansion:</strong> Feed your rough sections to AI one at a time: <em>"Here's my rough draft of this section. Expand it with more detail, smoother transitions, and a concrete example. Keep my voice — don't make it formal or corporate."</em></p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong style="color:var(--green)">Layer 3 — The edit pass:</strong> Paste the full draft back and ask: <em>"Edit this for clarity, flow, and impact. Cut anything repetitive. Strengthen weak transitions. Flag any sections that feel generic — I'll rewrite those with personal examples."</em></p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Structure</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Format for humans, not robots.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">People scan before they read. Your formatting needs to reward both behaviors:</p>
  <p class="section-text">Use <strong>subheadings every 200-300 words</strong> so scanners can jump to what interests them. Make each subheading a mini-promise — not "Section 3" but "The one thing most creators get wrong about hooks."</p>
  <p class="section-text">Keep paragraphs to <strong>2-3 sentences max</strong>. Big walls of text kill readership on screens. Use bold text for key phrases so the eye has anchor points. Drop in a short, punchy paragraph after a longer section to create rhythm.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Like this one.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section" style="background:var(--bg); border-left:3px solid var(--accent); padding:1.5rem; border-radius:8px;">
  <span class="section-label">Try It</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Write one blog post using the layered method.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Pick a topic you know well. Set a 45-minute timer. Spend 5 minutes brainstorming with AI, 10 minutes on the outline, 15 minutes on your rough draft (in your words), and 15 minutes on AI expansion and editing. Compare the result to how you used to write. Notice the difference in both speed and quality.</p>
</div>
