<div class="lesson-hero">
  <h1>Your Enterprise AI <span class="accent">Roadmap</span></h1>
  <p class="section-text">You have the frameworks. You understand the landscape, the business case, the data, the people, the vendors, the governance, the change management, and the metrics. Now it is time to put it all together into a 12-month roadmap that turns strategy into action. This is where everything converges.</p>
</div>

<div class="learn-card">
  <h3>What you will learn</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>How to structure a 12-month AI roadmap with quarterly milestones</li>
    <li>The sequencing that maximizes learning and minimizes risk</li>
    <li>How to build organizational momentum through strategic wins</li>
    <li>What separates organizations that scale AI from those that stall after pilots</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Quarter 1: Foundation</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Months 1-3: Prove It Works</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The first quarter is about building credibility through execution. Complete your readiness assessment. Select one high-impact, low-risk use case. Deploy a pilot with clear metrics. Get your data governance framework in place. Identify your AI champions. The goal is not transformation — it is evidence. Evidence that AI works in your organization, with your data, for your people.</p>
  <p class="section-text">End Q1 with a documented success story, a measured ROI, and a list of lessons learned. This becomes the foundation for everything that follows.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Quarter 2: Expand</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Months 4-6: Scale What Works</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Take your Q1 pilot and scale it. Expand to additional teams, departments, or use cases that share similar data and infrastructure. Begin your second pilot — something more ambitious that builds on what you learned. Invest in training. Your champions should be training their peers now. Start building the data infrastructure that supports multiple AI systems, not just one.</p>
  <p class="section-text">This is also when you formalize your vendor relationships and your governance framework. What worked informally in Q1 needs structure to survive Q2 and beyond.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Quarter 3: Operationalize</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Months 7-9: Make It Permanent</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Shift from projects to products. Your AI systems should have dedicated owners, SLAs, monitoring, and improvement cycles. Build your MLOps pipeline so new models can be deployed safely and quickly. Move from hub-and-spoke organization to embedded AI capability in business units. Launch your AI impact dashboard and begin regular reporting to leadership.</p>
  <p class="section-text">By the end of Q3, AI should feel like a normal part of how work gets done — not a special project. That normalization is the signal that transformation is taking root.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Quarter 4: Transform</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Months 10-12: Think Bigger</h2>
  <p class="section-text">With operational AI capability established, Q4 is about strategic evolution. Evaluate your competitive position. What can you do with AI that your competitors cannot? Where can AI create entirely new revenue streams, products, or customer experiences? Begin planning your Year 2 roadmap based on real data from Year 1.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Present a comprehensive impact report to leadership. Not just metrics — the story of how your organization learned to work with AI. The culture shift. The capabilities built. The opportunities ahead. This report secures Year 2 investment and executive commitment.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Like One Perspective</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Strategy Is a Practice, Not a Document</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Your roadmap will change. Markets shift, technology evolves, your organization learns things you did not expect. The value of the roadmap is not the plan itself — it is the clarity of thinking that produced it and the discipline to revisit it. Review quarterly. Adjust without guilt. The organizations that thrive with AI are not the ones that predicted the future correctly. They are the ones that learned faster than everyone else.</p>
  <p class="section-text">You have everything you need to begin. Not next quarter. Not after one more assessment. Now. The best AI strategy is the one you start executing today.</p>
</div>
