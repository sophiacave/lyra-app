<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Governance Framework</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Three Tiers of Oversight</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Not every AI system needs the same level of governance. A content recommendation engine and a loan approval system carry fundamentally different risks. Classify your AI systems into three tiers.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong>Tier 1 — Low risk:</strong> Internal productivity tools, content suggestions, search optimization. Light oversight. Annual review. <strong>Tier 2 — Medium risk:</strong> Customer-facing systems, automated decisions with human override, predictive analytics. Quarterly review, bias testing, explainability requirements. <strong>Tier 3 — High risk:</strong> Systems affecting employment, credit, health, safety, or legal rights. Pre-deployment review, continuous monitoring, mandatory human oversight, full audit trail.</p>
  <p class="section-text">This tiered approach means you are not slowing down low-risk innovation with heavy-risk governance. Speed where it is safe. Caution where it matters.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Bias and Fairness</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The Responsibility You Cannot Outsource</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Every AI system inherits the biases in its training data and the assumptions of its designers. This is not a theoretical concern — it is a measurable, testable, fixable problem. Build bias testing into your deployment pipeline. Test across demographic groups. Measure disparate impact. Document your findings and your decisions.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Fairness is not a single metric. It is a set of choices about what kind of organization you want to be. Those choices should be made deliberately by humans, not accidentally by algorithms.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Regulatory Readiness</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Prepare Now, Comply Later</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The EU AI Act is here. Similar regulations are emerging globally. The organizations that will navigate this smoothly are the ones building governance infrastructure today. Maintain an AI system inventory. Document your risk assessments. Keep audit trails. Establish clear accountability chains. When regulation arrives, you want to be adjusting your existing framework — not building one from scratch under deadline pressure.</p>
</div>
