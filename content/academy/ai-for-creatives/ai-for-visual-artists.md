<!-- SECTION 1: THE VISUAL AI LANDSCAPE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Landscape</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The tools are evolving fast. Here's what matters right now.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The AI image generation space moves fast — tools that were cutting-edge six months ago might be obsolete today. Rather than memorize every tool, understand the <strong>categories</strong> and what each is best for. Then you can adapt as the tools change.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🎨</div>
      <h4>Midjourney</h4>
      <p>The aesthetics champion. Produces the most visually polished output. Strong at illustration, concept art, and atmospheric imagery. Runs through Discord. Best for work that needs to <strong>look beautiful</strong> out of the box.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🖼️</div>
      <h4>DALL-E (via ChatGPT)</h4>
      <p>The most accessible option — built right into ChatGPT. Great for quick concepts, text in images, and iterative exploration. Less stylistically distinctive than Midjourney, but <strong>easier to control and iterate</strong>.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">⚙️</div>
      <h4>Stable Diffusion</h4>
      <p>The open-source powerhouse. Runs locally on your machine. Maximum control, custom models, ControlNet for precise guidance. Steeper learning curve but <strong>unlimited creative freedom</strong> and no per-image costs.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">✏️</div>
      <h4>Adobe Firefly & Built-In AI</h4>
      <p>AI integrated directly into Photoshop, Illustrator, and other tools you already use. Generative fill, extend, and recolor. Less about generating from scratch, more about <strong>augmenting your existing workflow</strong>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">The Real Advice</div>
    <p>Don't try to master every tool. <strong>Pick one that fits your workflow and go deep.</strong> A designer who knows Midjourney inside out will produce better work than someone who's dabbled in five tools. Once you understand prompt engineering for visuals, the skills transfer across platforms.</p>
  </div>
</div>

AI generates options at a speed that lets you <strong>see</strong> ideas instead of imagining them. You spend your energy curating and refining instead of producing every variation by hand.</div>

<!-- SECTION 3: VISUAL PROMPT ENGINEERING -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Craft</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Prompt engineering for visual output.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Writing prompts for images is a different skill than writing prompts for text. It's closer to art direction than writing. Here's the framework that produces consistently better results.</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The Visual Prompt Formula:</strong> Subject + Style + Mood + Technical Details + What to Avoid</p>
  </div>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:16px">
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">Subject (What)</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Be specific about what's in the image. "A woman reading" is vague. "A woman in her 60s reading a paperback at a window seat in a rain-streaked cafe" gives AI something to work with.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">Style (How)</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Reference art movements, artists, mediums, or visual styles. "Watercolor illustration," "film noir lighting," "in the style of Moebius," "35mm film grain," "vector flat design." The more specific your reference, the more controlled the output.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">Mood (Feel)</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">This is where creative prompting shines. "Melancholic but not sad," "tense and electric," "warm nostalgia with an undercurrent of unease." Mood words do more heavy lifting in image prompts than most people realize.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">Technical Details</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Lighting (golden hour, dramatic side light, overcast diffused), camera angle (bird's eye, low angle, close-up), color palette (muted earth tones, neon and black, monochrome with one accent color), composition (rule of thirds, centered symmetry, negative space).</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">Negative Prompts (What to Avoid)</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Equally important. "No text," "no watermark," "avoid photorealism," "don't make it look like stock photography," "no oversaturated colors." Telling AI what NOT to do prevents the most common failures.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text">Here's a complete example putting it all together:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="font-family:monospace;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.6">
      "An elderly Japanese craftsman working in a tiny ceramics studio. Surrounded by unfinished bowls and tools. Warm afternoon light from a single window. Shot from slightly above, like you're standing in the doorway watching. Style: documentary photography, slightly desaturated, film grain. Mood: quiet devotion, the beauty of repetition. No text, no watermark, not overly staged or posed."
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Advanced Move</div>
    <p><strong>Iterate with specifics, not "make it better."</strong> Instead of "try again," say exactly what to change: "More dramatic shadows. Pull the camera closer. Make the background darker." Treat it like art direction — the more specific your feedback, the closer AI gets to your vision.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Ethics</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Style, attribution, and originality: the conversation we need to have.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI image generation has created real ethical questions that every visual creative needs to think about. Not because there are easy answers, but because <strong>your creative integrity depends on engaging with these questions honestly</strong>.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:16px">
      <div style="padding:1rem;background:var(--bg);border-left:3px solid var(--orange);border-radius:0 10px 10px 0">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;margin-bottom:4px">Style Mimicry</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">AI can generate images "in the style of" specific living artists. Just because you can doesn't mean you should. Mimicking a living artist's distinctive style without credit is ethically questionable at best. Use style references for general directions ("art nouveau," "brutalist"), not to clone someone's livelihood.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:1rem;background:var(--bg);border-left:3px solid var(--orange);border-radius:0 10px 10px 0">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;margin-bottom:4px">Training Data & Consent</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">Most image models were trained on billions of images scraped from the internet — many without the creators' knowledge or consent. This is a real issue. Some artists have opted out. Some are suing. Being aware of where your tools come from is part of being a responsible creative professional.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:1rem;background:var(--bg);border-left:3px solid var(--orange);border-radius:0 10px 10px 0">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;margin-bottom:4px">Disclosure & Transparency</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">When is it okay to present AI-generated or AI-assisted work as your own? The creative community is still figuring this out. A good rule: if AI was a significant part of the creation process, say so. Transparency builds trust. Deception destroys it.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:1rem;background:var(--bg);border-left:3px solid var(--orange);border-radius:0 10px 10px 0">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;margin-bottom:4px">Commercial Use & Copyright</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">The legal landscape is still evolving. Generally, purely AI-generated images may not be copyrightable (you can't own something with no human authorship). But images where AI is one tool in a larger creative process — with significant human direction, selection, and modification — are on stronger ground. Check the terms of service for your specific tools.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The bottom line:</strong> Use AI as a tool in your creative process, not as a replacement for it. The more human involvement — direction, curation, modification, combination with traditional techniques — the stronger your ethical and legal position. And frankly, the better the work will be.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 5: PRACTICAL WORKFLOW -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Put It Together</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">A practical AI-assisted design workflow.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Here's a workflow you can start using on your next project. It works for graphic design, illustration, branding, or any visual discipline.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">01</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Brief & Direction</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Define the project in your own words. What's the feeling? Who's it for? What are the constraints? This is human work — AI can't do this for you.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">02</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">AI Exploration (15 min)</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Generate 20-30 images across different directions. Don't judge yet — just explore. Try different styles, moods, compositions. Cast a wide net.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">03</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Curate & Select (10 min)</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Spread out everything you generated. Pick the 3-5 that spark something. What do they have in common? That's your direction revealing itself through your taste.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">04</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Refine with AI (20 min)</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Take your selected direction and iterate. "More of this, less of that." Use image-to-image, inpainting, or upscaling to get closer to your vision. This is the conversation phase.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">05</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Human Finishing (varies)</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Bring the AI output into your design tools. Adjust, combine, redraw, typeset, compose. This is where your skills make AI output into real work. The final piece should be unmistakably yours.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Reality Check</div>
    <p>AI-generated images almost never come out perfect. They're raw material. <strong>The magic is in what you do with them after generation.</strong> Think of AI output as the digital equivalent of a sketch — it captures an idea, but the finished piece requires your hands, your eye, and your skill.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- KEY TAKEAWAY -->
<div class="callout purple">
  <p><strong>The key insight:</strong> AI image generation doesn't threaten great designers — it threatens mediocre stock photography and template-based design. If your value is in your <strong>eye, your taste, and your ability to solve visual problems</strong>, AI makes you faster and more exploratory. If your value was in production speed alone, yes, that's changing. The move is clear: lean into what makes you irreplaceable — your creative judgment — and let AI handle the volume.</p>
