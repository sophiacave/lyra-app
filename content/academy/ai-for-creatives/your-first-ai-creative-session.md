<!-- SECTION 1: CREATIVE PROMPTING IS DIFFERENT -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Difference</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Creative prompting is nothing like corporate prompting.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Most prompt guides are written for business use. "Be specific." "Give context." "Define the output format." That advice is fine for generating a quarterly report summary. It's terrible for creative work.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Creative prompting is more like <strong>giving direction to a talented collaborator</strong>. You don't hand them a spec sheet — you share a feeling, an intention, a vibe. You say things like "I want this to feel like driving alone at 2am" or "Think Cormac McCarthy meets a Wes Anderson color palette."</p>
  <p class="section-text">The best creative prompts are <strong>evocative, not prescriptive</strong>. They leave room for surprise. They invite the AI to bring something you didn't expect.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.5rem">😐</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;color:var(--muted)">Corporate Prompt</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">"Write a 500-word blog post about sustainable fashion. Target audience: women 25-40. Tone: professional. Include 3 statistics."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.5rem">🔥</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;color:var(--orange)">Creative Prompt</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">"I want to write something about sustainable fashion that doesn't sound like every other article. Something that makes a 30-year-old woman stop scrolling. Raw. A little angry. Like talking to a friend who finally gets it. What if we started with the worst outfit she ever bought and worked backwards?"</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text">See the difference? The creative prompt shares <strong>energy, emotion, and a starting image</strong>. It doesn't constrain — it inspires. And the output will be wildly different because of it.</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The secret:</strong> Talk to AI the way you'd talk to a creative partner at a coffee shop. Not the way you'd fill out a form. The more human your prompt, the more human the output.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2: THE CREATIVE CONVERSATION -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Method</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">How to have a creative conversation with AI.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The best AI creative sessions aren't one prompt and done. They're <strong>conversations</strong>. Back and forth. Building on each other. Getting closer to the thing you can feel but can't quite articulate yet.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Here's the flow that works:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:16px">
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">1</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Open with energy, not instructions.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">"I'm working on a short story about a woman who discovers her grandmother was a spy. I want it to feel intimate — like reading someone's diary. Literary fiction, not thriller."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">2</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">React honestly to what AI gives you.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">"I love the opening image but the dialogue feels too formal. Make her messier. She talks the way people actually talk — half sentences, interrupting herself."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">3</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Use references and comparisons.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">"Think Ocean Vuong's sentence rhythms but with the dark humor of Fleabag. And the grandmother should feel like a character from a Miyazaki film — fierce but tender."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">4</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Push further. Ask for the unexpected.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">"This is good but safe. Give me a version that takes a risk. Something that would make a writing workshop uncomfortable. Surprise me."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">5</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Steal the best parts. Discard the rest.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">You're not looking for a finished piece. You're mining for gold. One perfect sentence. One unexpected angle. One structural idea you wouldn't have found alone.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Pro Tip</div>
    <p>Don't accept the first output. <strong>The magic is always in the third or fourth exchange.</strong> The first response is AI warming up. The good stuff comes when you push, redirect, and react. Treat it like a jam session — the best riff comes after you've been playing for a while.</p>
  </div>

<!-- SECTION 3: BEATING THE BLANK PAGE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Blank Page</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">AI is the best cure for creative paralysis.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Every creative knows the blank page. The cursor blinking. The canvas staring. The instrument sitting there, waiting. You know you have something in you, but the gap between <strong>nothing</strong> and <strong>something</strong> feels impossible.</p>
  <p class="section-text">AI closes that gap instantly. Not by doing your work for you — but by giving you <strong>something to react to</strong>. And reacting is infinitely easier than creating from nothing.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">💥</div>
      <h4>The Explosion Method</h4>
      <p>"Give me 15 wildly different opening lines for a story about [topic]. Some serious, some funny, some weird. Don't play it safe."</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🔀</div>
      <h4>The Collision Method</h4>
      <p>"What if [thing A] met [thing B]? Like, what would a children's book about quantum physics actually look like? Give me 5 concepts."</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🔄</div>
      <h4>The Flip Method</h4>
      <p>"Take the most cliched version of [genre/format] and turn it completely upside down. What's the opposite of what everyone expects?"</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🎭</div>
      <h4>The Character Method</h4>
      <p>"Describe this [project/concept] from the perspective of someone who absolutely hates it, someone who loves it, and someone who's confused by it."</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text">The point isn't that any of these outputs will be your final work. The point is that <strong>now you have something to respond to</strong>. Your creative brain kicks in. "No, not that — but what if..." And suddenly the blank page isn't blank anymore.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: PROMPT TEMPLATES FOR CREATIVES -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Your Toolkit</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Prompt starters that actually work for creative people.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Copy these. Modify them. Make them yours. These are <strong>starting points</strong>, not formulas. The best prompt is always the one that sounds like you.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:16px">
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For brainstorming</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"I'm exploring [topic/theme]. Don't give me the obvious angles — I want the ones that make me think. Give me 10 directions, ranked from safe to genuinely surprising."</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For finding your voice</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"Here's a paragraph I wrote: [paste]. Now write the same idea in 5 different voices — one poetic, one punk, one academic, one like a TikTok script, one like a love letter. I want to see which one feels most like me."</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For getting unstuck</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"I'm stuck on [project]. Here's where I am: [describe]. I don't want you to finish it — I want you to ask me 5 questions about it that will help me figure out what's missing."</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For iteration</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"Here's my draft: [paste]. Give me three versions — one that's tighter, one that's bolder, and one that breaks the rules entirely. Keep my voice but push me further."</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For feedback</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"Read this as a [editor / art director / music producer / target audience member]. What works? What doesn't? Be honest — I'd rather hear it now than after I publish."</div>
      </div>
    </div>

<!-- SECTION 5: HANDS-ON EXERCISE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Try It Now</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Exercise: From one brief to five concepts in five minutes.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Here's a creative brief. Your job is to use AI to generate 5 completely different creative concepts from it. Not one concept five ways — five genuinely different directions.</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The Brief:</strong> A independent coffee shop wants to stand out in a neighborhood full of chains. They don't have a big budget. They want something that feels authentic, a little rebellious, and makes people want to come back. Could be a tagline, a campaign concept, a social media series, a mural, an event — anything creative.</p>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text">Open Claude (or your AI tool of choice) and try this prompt:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="font-family:monospace;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.6">
      "I'm brainstorming for an independent coffee shop that wants to fight back against the chains in their neighborhood. Low budget, high attitude. Give me 5 completely different creative concepts — not variations of the same idea, but genuinely different approaches. For each one, give me: a name for the concept, a one-line description, and why it would work. Make them bold. At least one should make me nervous."
    </div>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text">After you get the response:</p>
  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:8px">
      <div style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim)"><strong>1.</strong> Pick the one that excites you most — even if it scares you a little.</div>
      <div style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim)"><strong>2.</strong> Ask AI to go deeper on that concept. "Expand on concept 3. Give me the full vision — what does it look like, feel like, sound like?"</div>
      <div style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim)"><strong>3.</strong> Push back on something. "I love this but the name feels too safe. Give me 10 name options that are edgier."</div>
      <div style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim)"><strong>4.</strong> Notice how the conversation shapes the output. <strong>You're directing. AI is generating. Together you're creating something neither of you would make alone.</strong></div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">What You Just Did</div>
    <p>In about 5 minutes, you generated more raw creative material than most brainstorming sessions produce in an hour. And you did it by <strong>having a conversation</strong>, not filling out a template. That's the skill this course is building: the ability to direct AI with creative intention. Everything from here builds on this foundation.</p>
  </div>
</div>

Your job is to be a great creative director. AI is the fastest collaborator you've ever had.</p>
