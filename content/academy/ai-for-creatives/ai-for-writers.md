<!-- SECTION 1: THE WRITER'S FEAR -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Big Question</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">If AI can write, why do they need me?</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Let's address this head-on. Yes, AI can write. It can produce grammatically correct, well-structured, reasonably coherent text on almost any topic. It can write a blog post, a product description, a short story, a poem.</p>
  <p class="section-text">And almost all of it sounds the same.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Read ten AI-generated blog posts in a row and you'll notice: the same rhythms, the same transitions, the same slightly-too-polished cadence. It's competent. It's correct. And it's <strong>forgettable</strong>.</p>
  <p class="section-text">What makes writing matter — what makes someone stop scrolling, lean forward, feel something — is <strong>voice</strong>. The way you break a rule on purpose. The sentence fragment that hits harder than any paragraph. The metaphor that could only come from someone who lived your specific life.</p>
  <p class="section-text">AI doesn't have that. You do. The question isn't whether AI can write. The question is: <strong>what can you create when your voice has the fastest pen in the world behind it?</strong></p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The writer's real advantage:</strong> AI has made competent writing free. That means competent writing is now worthless. What's valuable is writing with personality, perspective, and soul. If you have those — and you do, or you wouldn't be taking this course — AI makes you <em>more</em> valuable, not less.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2: MAINTAINING YOUR VOICE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Voice Protection</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">How to keep your voice while using AI.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">This is the single most important skill for writers using AI. Without it, everything you produce will sound like everything everyone else produces. Here's how to protect what makes your writing yours.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">📝</div>
      <h4>Feed It Your Writing First</h4>
      <p>Before asking AI to write anything, paste in 2-3 examples of YOUR writing. Say: "This is my voice. Match this tone, rhythm, and style in everything you write for me." AI can mimic voice remarkably well — if you give it the source.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">✂️</div>
      <h4>Use AI for Structure, You for Soul</h4>
      <p>Let AI build the outline, the structure, the flow. Then you write (or heavily rewrite) the actual sentences. The architecture is AI's. The language is yours.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🔍</div>
      <h4>Edit Ruthlessly</h4>
      <p>AI-generated text has tells: overuse of "delve," "it's important to note," "in conclusion." Read every line and ask: "Would I say this?" If not, rewrite it or cut it. Your editing eye IS your voice.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🎯</div>
      <h4>Name What You Don't Want</h4>
      <p>"Don't be formal. Don't use buzzwords. Don't start paragraphs with 'In today's world.' Don't hedge — if you're going to say something, say it." Negative instructions are incredibly powerful for voice.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">The Voice Test</div>
    <p>After using AI on any piece of writing, read it aloud. <strong>If it doesn't sound like you talking, it's not done.</strong> The moment you hear a sentence that feels "AI-ish" — too smooth, too safe, too generic — that's where your editing starts. Your ear knows your voice better than any prompt can describe it.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 3: THE TWO WORKFLOWS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Workflows</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Two ways to write with AI. Pick the right one.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">There's no single "correct" way to use AI for writing. But there are two distinct workflows, and knowing which one to use for which project will save you hours and produce better work.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:grid;grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr;gap:20px">
      <div style="padding:1rem;background:var(--bg);border:2px solid var(--orange);border-radius:12px">
        <div style="font-size:.75rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:.5rem;text-transform:uppercase;letter-spacing:1px">Workflow A: Human Creates, AI Refines</div>
        <p style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);margin-bottom:.75rem">You write the first draft. AI helps you edit, tighten, restructure, and polish. Your voice leads from the start.</p>
        <div style="font-size:.8rem;color:var(--muted)">
          <strong>Best for:</strong> Personal essays, literary fiction, opinion pieces, poetry, anything where voice is everything.
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:1rem;background:var(--bg);border:2px solid var(--green);border-radius:12px">
        <div style="font-size:.75rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--green);margin-bottom:.5rem;text-transform:uppercase;letter-spacing:1px">Workflow B: AI Drafts, Human Transforms</div>
        <p style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);margin-bottom:.75rem">AI generates the first draft or multiple options. You rewrite, curate, and inject your voice. Speed leads, voice follows.</p>
        <div style="font-size:.8rem;color:var(--muted)">
          <strong>Best for:</strong> Blog posts, marketing copy, product descriptions, social content, email sequences — anything where speed matters and volume is high.
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text">Most professional writers use <strong>both</strong> — sometimes in the same week. The novel gets Workflow A. The newsletter gets Workflow B. The client blog post might start with B and finish with A. There's no purity test here. Use what serves the work.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 5: THE EDITING PARTNERSHIP -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Secret Weapon</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">AI is the best editor you've never had to pay.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Here's something most writers discover by accident: <strong>AI is better at editing than it is at writing</strong>. Give it a piece of your writing and ask it to improve it, and the results are often remarkable. It can spot structural issues, pacing problems, unclear arguments, and weak openings with the ruthless efficiency of a seasoned editor.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The key is knowing how to ask for the right kind of editing.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.5rem">🔬</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Structural Edit</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">"Read this and tell me: Does the structure work? Where does the energy drop? What should be moved, cut, or expanded? Don't rewrite — just diagnose."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.5rem">✂️</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Line Edit</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">"Go through this line by line. Flag any sentence that's flabby, unclear, or trying too hard. Suggest tighter alternatives but keep my voice — don't make it sound like AI."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.5rem">🎯</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Audience Edit</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">"Read this as [specific audience — e.g., a skeptical CTO / a first-time novelist / a teenager]. What would confuse them? What would bore them? What would they skip?"</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.5rem">💎</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Polish Edit</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">"This is nearly done. Check for: repeated words, inconsistent tense, weak verbs, sentences that start the same way. Make it 10% better without changing the meaning."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The writer's workflow:</strong> Write your first draft yourself. It can be messy — that's fine. Then bring AI in as your editor. Get the structural notes. Do a tightening pass. Check it against your audience. Polish the final version. You've just compressed what used to be a multi-day editing process into an hour. And your voice stays front and center because <em>you wrote the original</em>.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 6: COMMON MISTAKES -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Watch Out</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Mistakes writers make with AI (and how to avoid them).</h2>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="color:var(--red);font-weight:700;font-size:1rem;flex-shrink:0">01</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Publishing the first draft.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">AI's first output is a starting point, never a final product. If you publish without rewriting, you're publishing generic work with your name on it. Always add your fingerprints.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="color:var(--red);font-weight:700;font-size:1rem;flex-shrink:0">02</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Letting AI smooth out your edges.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">AI tends to "fix" things that aren't broken — your intentional fragments, your weird rhythms, your rule-breaking. Tell it explicitly: "Don't smooth out my style. The rough parts are intentional."</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="color:var(--red);font-weight:700;font-size:1rem;flex-shrink:0">03</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Using AI as a crutch instead of a tool.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">If you stop writing first drafts entirely, your writing muscles atrophy. Use AI to amplify your writing practice, not replace it. The best AI-assisted writers are also the best unassisted writers.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="color:var(--red);font-weight:700;font-size:1rem;flex-shrink:0">04</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Not fact-checking AI's claims.</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">AI will confidently cite statistics that don't exist, attribute quotes to the wrong people, and present invented facts as truth. If it's going in your published work, verify it. Your reputation is on the line.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- KEY TAKEAWAY -->
<div class="callout purple">
  <p><strong>The key insight:</strong> AI is the most powerful writing tool since the word processor. But just like a word processor doesn't make you a writer, AI doesn't make you a writer. <strong>Your voice, your perspective, your willingness to say something real — that's what makes you a writer.</strong> AI just removes the friction between your ideas and the page. Use it to write more, write faster, and write bolder. Never use it to write less like yourself.</p>
