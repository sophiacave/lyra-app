<!-- ── COURSE RECAP: YOUR CREATIVE AI JOURNEY ── -->
<div class="learn-card">
  <h3 style="color:#34d399;">Course Recap: Your Creative AI Journey</h3>
  <p>Before you tackle the final assessment, let's revisit the core concepts you've built across all nine lessons. These aren't just theory — they're the tools you'll use every day as a creative who works with AI.</p>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(3, 1fr); gap:1rem; margin:1.5rem 0;">

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #34d399; border-radius:10px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#34d399; margin-top:0;">The 3-Question Filter</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">Every AI tool must pass three tests before it earns a spot in your stack: <strong>(1)</strong> Does it solve a real bottleneck? <strong>(2)</strong> Can I learn it in under an hour? <strong>(3)</strong> Will I use it at least weekly? If any answer is no, skip it. This filter prevents shiny-object syndrome and keeps your toolkit lean.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #8b5cf6; border-radius:10px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#8b5cf6; margin-top:0;">Style Anchors &amp; Voice Preservation</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">Your voice is your fingerprint. A style anchor is a reusable description of your writing voice — created by feeding AI 3-5 samples of your best work and asking it to analyze your patterns. Attach it to every prompt so output sounds like <em>you</em>, not a generic chatbot.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #fb923c; border-radius:10px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#fb923c; margin-top:0;">Iterative Prompting</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">One-shot prompting is the beginner's trap. The real magic happens in conversation — pushing, redirecting, and reacting across multiple exchanges. Your best output almost always emerges on the <strong>3rd or 4th exchange</strong>, not the first.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #38bdf8; border-radius:10px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#38bdf8; margin-top:0;">Content Pipelines &amp; Batch Creation</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">Batch by format, not by day. Write all captions in one session, all graphics briefs in another. This keeps you and AI in the same creative mode, producing more consistent voice and quality. A well-structured pipeline turns a month of content into a single focused afternoon.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #f472b6; border-radius:10px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#f472b6; margin-top:0;">The Quality Gate</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">Five checks before anything goes live: <strong>Voice check</strong> — sounds like you? <strong>Value check</strong> — worth bookmarking? <strong>Fact check</strong> — claims verified? <strong>Platform check</strong> — formatted right? <strong>Cringe check</strong> — no regrets tomorrow? If it fails any gate, it goes back to editing.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #a1a1aa; border-radius:10px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#a1a1aa; margin-top:0;">Ethics, Copyright &amp; Disclosure</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">Transparency builds trust. The professional standard: disclose that you use AI for drafting and research, emphasize that all final work is reviewed and approved by you. Copyright law is evolving — always add enough original creative expression that the final product is unmistakably yours.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- ── THE CREATIVE'S AI TOOLKIT DECISION FRAMEWORK ── -->
<div class="learn-card">
  <h3 style="color:#8b5cf6;">The Creative's AI Toolkit Decision Framework</h3>
  <p>Not everything should be handed to AI. Not everything should be done manually. The skill is knowing which is which.</p>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr; gap:1.5rem; margin:1.5rem 0;">

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #34d399; border-radius:10px; padding:1.25rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#34d399; margin-top:0;">When AI Shines</h4>
      <ul style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; line-height:1.7;">
        <li><strong>Brainstorming &amp; ideation</strong> — generating 20 angles on a topic in seconds</li>
        <li><strong>First drafts</strong> — getting past the blank page fast</li>
        <li><strong>Research synthesis</strong> — summarizing sources, extracting key points</li>
        <li><strong>Format conversion</strong> — turning a blog post into tweets, a podcast into show notes</li>
        <li><strong>Batch production</strong> — creating variations, A/B options, multi-platform versions</li>
        <li><strong>Structural editing</strong> — reorganizing, tightening, finding logical gaps</li>
        <li><strong>Overcoming creative blocks</strong> — when you're stuck, AI can unstick you</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #ef4444; border-radius:10px; padding:1.25rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#ef4444; margin-top:0;">When You Do It Yourself</h4>
      <ul style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; line-height:1.7;">
        <li><strong>Original creative vision</strong> — the core idea must come from you</li>
        <li><strong>Taste &amp; editorial judgment</strong> — deciding what's good enough is a human skill</li>
        <li><strong>Brand voice nuance</strong> — AI approximates voice; you <em>are</em> the voice</li>
        <li><strong>Emotional authenticity</strong> — personal stories, vulnerability, lived experience</li>
        <li><strong>Relationship building</strong> — replies, DMs, community interaction</li>
        <li><strong>Strategic decisions</strong> — what to create, who to serve, where to publish</li>
        <li><strong>Final approval</strong> — nothing ships without your eyes on it</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:2px solid #8b5cf6; border-radius:10px; padding:1.25rem; margin-top:0.5rem; text-align:center;">
    <p style="color:#8b5cf6; font-size:1.1rem; font-weight:bold; margin:0;">The Golden Rule: AI assists, you decide.</p>
    <p style="color:#a1a1aa; font-size:0.9rem; margin:0.5rem 0 0 0;">AI is the most powerful creative assistant ever built. But an assistant that makes all the decisions is not an assistant — it's a replacement. Keep your hands on the wheel.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- ── CREATIVE AI RED FLAGS ── -->
<div class="learn-card">
  <h3 style="color:#ef4444;">Creative AI Red Flags</h3>
  <p>AI is a tool that can sharpen your work — or slowly erode it. These are the warning signs that your relationship with AI has drifted from collaboration to dependency. If you spot any of these in your own practice, it's time to recalibrate.</p>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr; gap:1rem; margin:1.5rem 0;">

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-left:4px solid #ef4444; border-radius:6px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#ef4444; margin-top:0;">Your audience says your work feels different</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">And they don't mean it as a compliment. This is the clearest sign of <strong>homogenization</strong> — AI's default voice patterns are overwriting yours. Your audience followed you for <em>your</em> voice. If they're noticing a shift, AI defaults are bleeding through your editing process.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-left:4px solid #fb923c; border-radius:6px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#fb923c; margin-top:0;">You skip the editing step</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">When AI output goes straight to publish without meaningful human editing, you've become a prompt jockey, not a creator. The editing phase — where you cut, rewrite, add your perspective, and apply taste — is where your value lives. Skip it and you're just a middleman.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-left:4px solid #f472b6; border-radius:6px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#f472b6; margin-top:0;">You can't explain your creative choices</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">If someone asks why you structured a piece a certain way, chose that angle, or used that metaphor — and your honest answer is "the AI did it" — you've outsourced your creative judgment. You should be able to defend every major choice in your work, because <em>you</em> made it.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-left:4px solid #38bdf8; border-radius:6px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#38bdf8; margin-top:0;">You use the same prompts for every piece</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">A prompt library is great. A prompt <em>rut</em> is not. If every blog post, every caption, every email starts with the same template — your output will converge toward sameness. Good prompts evolve with your work. They should be living documents, not copy-paste rituals.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-left:4px solid #8b5cf6; border-radius:6px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#8b5cf6; margin-top:0;">You've stopped developing your own skills</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">If you no longer practice writing, designing, or creating without AI — your foundational skills are atrophying. AI amplifies what you bring to the table. If you stop bringing anything, there's nothing to amplify. The best AI-augmented creatives are the ones who could do the work without AI — they just choose not to for efficiency.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-left:4px solid #a1a1aa; border-radius:6px; padding:1rem;">
      <h4 style="color:#a1a1aa; margin-top:0;">You measure success by volume alone</h4>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem;">AI makes it trivially easy to produce <em>more</em>. But more is not better if quality drops. If your primary metric has shifted from "did this resonate?" to "did I hit my posting quota?" — you're optimizing for the wrong thing. One piece that moves people beats ten that scroll past.</p>
    </div>

  </div>

  <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:2px solid #ef4444; border-radius:10px; padding:1.25rem; margin-top:0.5rem;">
    <p style="color:#ef4444; font-size:1rem; font-weight:bold; margin:0 0 0.5rem 0;">The Recalibration Test</p>
    <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; margin:0;">If you spotted yourself in two or more of these red flags, try this: create your next piece entirely without AI. If it's painful, that's the muscle atrophy talking. If it's liberating, you've been leaning on AI too heavily. Either way, the exercise reconnects you with your creative instincts — the thing that makes your work <em>yours</em>.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- ── PRE-ASSESSMENT WARM-UP ── -->
<div class="learn-card">
  <h3 style="color:#38bdf8;">Pre-Assessment Warm-Up</h3>
  <p>Before you dive into the flashcards and quiz below, ground yourself in these key principles. They represent the through-line of everything you've learned in this course.</p>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr 1fr; gap:1rem; margin:1.5rem 0;">

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-bottom:3px solid #34d399; border-radius:8px; padding:1rem; text-align:center;">
      <p style="color:#34d399; font-size:1.5rem; margin:0;">1</p>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; margin:0.5rem 0 0 0;"><strong>Tools serve workflows.</strong> Never adopt a tool without a bottleneck to solve. The 3-Question Filter is your permanent gatekeeper.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-bottom:3px solid #8b5cf6; border-radius:8px; padding:1rem; text-align:center;">
      <p style="color:#8b5cf6; font-size:1.5rem; margin:0;">2</p>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; margin:0.5rem 0 0 0;"><strong>Voice is non-negotiable.</strong> Your style anchor protects your identity. Feed it into every prompt. If AI output doesn't sound like you, it doesn't ship.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-bottom:3px solid #fb923c; border-radius:8px; padding:1rem; text-align:center;">
      <p style="color:#fb923c; font-size:1.5rem; margin:0;">3</p>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; margin:0.5rem 0 0 0;"><strong>Conversation beats commands.</strong> Iterative prompting — pushing, redirecting, refining — produces dramatically better output than any single prompt.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-bottom:3px solid #f472b6; border-radius:8px; padding:1rem; text-align:center;">
      <p style="color:#f472b6; font-size:1.5rem; margin:0;">4</p>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; margin:0.5rem 0 0 0;"><strong>Systems beat sprints.</strong> Content pipelines and batch creation turn chaos into consistency. Structure your process and AI becomes predictably useful.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-bottom:3px solid #ef4444; border-radius:8px; padding:1rem; text-align:center;">
      <p style="color:#ef4444; font-size:1.5rem; margin:0;">5</p>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; margin:0.5rem 0 0 0;"><strong>The Quality Gate is sacred.</strong> Voice, value, fact, platform, cringe — five checks, every time. No exceptions. This is what separates professionals from prompt jockeys.</p>
    </div>

    <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border-bottom:3px solid #38bdf8; border-radius:8px; padding:1rem; text-align:center;">
      <p style="color:#38bdf8; font-size:1.5rem; margin:0;">6</p>
      <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:0.92rem; margin:0.5rem 0 0 0;"><strong>Transparency is strength.</strong> Disclose AI use honestly. The audience respects the process when you own it. Secrecy erodes trust faster than any tool choice.</p>
    </div>

  </div>

  <div style="background:#1a1a2e; border:1px solid #71717a; border-radius:10px; padding:1.25rem; margin-top:0.5rem; text-align:center;">
    <p style="color:#e5e5e5; font-size:1rem; margin:0;"><strong style="color:#38bdf8;">Remember:</strong> The quiz tests application, not memorization. You'll be given scenarios and asked to make decisions — just like you would in real creative work. Think about <em>why</em> each principle exists, not just what it says.</p>
  </div>
