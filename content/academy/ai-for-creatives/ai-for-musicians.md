<!-- SECTION 1: THE MUSIC AI LANDSCAPE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Landscape</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">AI music tools are wild right now. Here's what actually works.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Music was one of the last creative domains to get hit by AI — and when it did, it hit hard. In the span of about 18 months, we went from novelty jingles to AI-generated tracks that make professional producers do a double-take. But like all AI tools, <strong>the reality is more nuanced than the headlines</strong>.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Here's an honest breakdown of where things stand.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🎵</div>
      <h4>Suno</h4>
      <p>The most accessible text-to-music tool. Describe what you want in plain English and get a full track with vocals in under a minute. Impressive for demos, brainstorming, and quick concepts. Quality varies — sometimes magical, sometimes uncanny valley.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🎶</div>
      <h4>Udio</h4>
      <p>Similar to Suno but often produces more musically sophisticated output, especially for genres like jazz, classical, and soul. Excellent for exploring genre combinations and melodic ideas. Strong vocal generation.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🎹</div>
      <h4>AI DAW Plugins</h4>
      <p>Tools like AIVA, Amper, and AI features built into Ableton, Logic, and FL Studio. These work inside your existing production environment — generating MIDI patterns, suggesting chord progressions, creating drum fills. More control, more integration with your workflow.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🔧</div>
      <h4>Open-Source Models</h4>
      <p>MusicGen (Meta), Riffusion, and others. Run locally, fully customizable, no usage limits. Best for technical musicians who want maximum control. The quality gap with commercial tools is closing fast.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>Honest take:</strong> As of right now, AI can produce music that sounds <em>good enough</em> for background, demos, and content. But it struggles with the things that make music <em>great</em>: genuine emotional dynamics, intentional imperfection, the tension between what's expected and what actually happens. That gap is where musicians live — and it's not closing as fast as the tech press thinks.</p>
  </div>

<!-- SECTION 2: AI FOR COMPOSITION -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Composition</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Using AI as a songwriting partner.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">This is where AI is most immediately useful for working musicians. Not as a replacement for your creative instinct, but as a <strong>brainstorming partner that never runs out of ideas</strong>.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:16px">
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">1</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Chord Progression Exploration</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">Ask Claude: "Give me 5 chord progressions that feel like driving at sunset — melancholy but not depressing. Key of D major. I want at least one borrowed chord in each." You'll get harmonic ideas in seconds that might take hours of noodling to discover. Not all will be gold — but one might unlock the whole song.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">2</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Melody Ideation</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">Use AI music tools to generate melodic ideas over your chord progression. Suno and Udio can produce vocal melodies; MIDI-based tools give you notation you can import into your DAW. Think of these as audible sketches — something to react to, build on, or push against.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">3</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Arrangement Inspiration</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">"I have a verse and chorus. The verse is acoustic guitar and voice. What instruments or textures could enter in the chorus to create lift without getting loud?" AI can suggest arrangements, instrument combinations, and production techniques you might not have considered.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:1rem;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:var(--orange);color:var(--bg-deep);width:28px;height:28px;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:.8rem;flex-shrink:0">4</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Genre Mashups & Experimentation</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;margin-top:4px">"What would happen if you took a bossa nova rhythm, added trap hi-hats, and put a shoegaze guitar wall over it?" AI tools can generate these wild combinations instantly. Most will be terrible. Some will be genuinely innovative. All of them expand your sonic palette.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

<!-- SECTION 3: AI FOR LYRICS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Lyrics</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">AI + lyrics: your writing room that never sleeps.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Lyrics are where AI can be genuinely transformative for musicians — not because AI writes great lyrics (it usually doesn't), but because it's an <strong>inexhaustible brainstorming partner</strong> for the most mentally taxing part of songwriting.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:16px">
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For rhyme and rhythm work</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"I need words that rhyme with 'borderline' but aren't cliche. Give me 15 options — some perfect rhymes, some slant rhymes. Bonus points for words with two or three syllables that have a hard consonant at the end."</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For imagery and metaphor</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"I'm writing a song about leaving a toxic relationship. Give me 10 metaphors that aren't about doors, cages, or storms. I want images that are physical, specific, and surprising. Think Phoebe Bridgers, not Hallmark."</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For structural alternatives</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"Here's my verse: [paste]. The syllable count works but it doesn't sing well — too many consonant clusters. Rewrite it with the same meaning but smoother vowel sounds. Keep the imagery, change the words."</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--orange);margin-bottom:4px">For getting unstuck on a bridge</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem;font-family:monospace">"My verse is about being numb. My chorus is about finally feeling something. I need a bridge that connects these — a moment of transition. Give me 5 approaches. At least one should shift perspective (maybe second person, maybe a memory)."</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">The Lyricist's Rule</div>
    <p><strong>Never use an AI-generated line verbatim unless it genuinely moves you.</strong> AI lyrics tend to be competent but generic. The value is in the sparks — a word you hadn't considered, a rhyme scheme that opens a door, an image that triggers something real in you. Mine for those sparks, then write the actual lyrics yourself.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: VOICE SYNTHESIS & AUDIO AI -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The New Frontier</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Text-to-speech, voice cloning, and the audio revolution.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Beyond music generation, AI is transforming the broader audio landscape in ways that matter for musicians, podcasters, voiceover artists, and audio producers.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🗣️</div>
      <h4>Text-to-Speech (TTS)</h4>
      <p>Modern TTS is shockingly good. Tools like ElevenLabs and OpenAI's voice API produce speech that's nearly indistinguishable from human recordings. Use cases: narration, podcast intros, demo vocals, multilingual content, accessibility.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🎤</div>
      <h4>Voice Cloning</h4>
      <p>Clone your own voice and use it to generate spoken content without re-recording. Record 30 seconds, get a synthetic version of your voice for demos, prototypes, and placeholder audio. Powerful — and requires ethical responsibility.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🔊</div>
      <h4>Audio Enhancement</h4>
      <p>AI noise removal (like Adobe Podcast's "Enhance Speech"), automatic mastering (LANDR, iZotope), stem separation (Lalal.ai, Demucs). These tools handle technical audio work that used to require expensive studios and specialized engineers.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">🎧</div>
      <h4>Sound Design</h4>
      <p>AI can generate sound effects, ambient textures, and foley from text descriptions. "The sound of rain on a tin roof with distant thunder and a ticking clock." Useful for film scoring, game audio, podcasts, and immersive experiences.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The ethics note:</strong> Voice cloning technology is powerful and easily abused. Never clone someone else's voice without their explicit consent. Never use AI-generated voices to impersonate real people. The same principle applies here as everywhere in this course: <strong>use the power responsibly, and be transparent about when AI is involved.</strong></p>
  </div>

</div>

<!-- SECTION 5: BUILDING YOUR WORKFLOW -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Your Workflow</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Building an AI-assisted music workflow.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Here's a practical workflow for integrating AI into your music production process. Adapt it to your genre, your tools, and your creative style.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">01</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Ideation Phase: Cast a Wide Net</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Use AI to explore. Generate 10 chord progressions. Ask for genre-bending combinations. Create 5 demo tracks in Suno/Udio to hear different directions. Use Claude for lyric brainstorming. <strong>Goal: quantity, not quality.</strong> You're looking for sparks.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">02</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Selection Phase: Trust Your Ear</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Listen to everything. What made you lean forward? What phrase stuck in your head? What chord change gave you chills? <strong>Your taste is the filter.</strong> Pick the best elements — a progression from here, a melodic idea from there, a lyric concept from this one.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">03</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Creation Phase: Your Hands on the Instrument</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Take the sparks into your DAW, your instrument, your voice. This is where human musicianship takes over. Play the progressions. Sing the melodies. Write the lyrics. AI gave you the starting material — you make it music.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">04</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Production Phase: AI as Your Engineer</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">Use AI plugins for drum programming, synth patches, and arrangement suggestions. Let AI handle noise removal, vocal tuning, and rough mixing. Use stem separation to sample and remix. <strong>AI handles the technical; you handle the creative.</strong></div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;align-items:center;gap:1rem;padding:.75rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <span style="font-size:1.2rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--orange)">05</span>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem">Polish Phase: Human Ears, Final Call</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem">AI-assisted mastering can get you 80% of the way. The last 20% is your ears, your monitors, and your understanding of how the track should feel. Listen on headphones, in the car, on your phone. The final decisions are yours.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">For Independent Musicians</div>
    <p>Here's the real game-changer: AI has <strong>dramatically lowered the barrier to full production</strong>. A bedroom musician can now produce tracks that sound professional without a studio, a band, or a producer. That doesn't mean the music is automatically good — it still needs taste, vision, and emotional truth. But the technical barriers? They're crumbling. If you have something to say, the tools to say it are now in your hands.</p>
  </div>

<!-- KEY TAKEAWAY -->
<div class="callout purple">
  <p><strong>The key insight:</strong> Music is the most emotional art form humans have. AI can generate sounds, patterns, and even convincing performances. But it can't feel what it means to play a note with intention, to hold a silence one beat longer than expected, to choose the imperfect take because it's <em>more true</em>. <strong>Your musicianship isn't your technical skill — it's your emotional intelligence expressed through sound.</strong> AI amplifies your capacity. It doesn't replace your soul. Use it to make more music, explore more ideas, and reach more people. The instrument has changed. What it means to play hasn't.</p>
