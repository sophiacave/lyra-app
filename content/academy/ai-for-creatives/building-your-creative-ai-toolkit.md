<!-- SECTION 1: CURATING TOOLS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Philosophy</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">More tools is not the answer. The right tools is.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">There are hundreds of AI creative tools out there right now. New ones launch every week. And the temptation is to try them all, sign up for everything, collect free trials like trading cards. <strong>That's a trap.</strong></p>
  <p class="section-text">The creatives who actually benefit from AI aren't using 15 tools. They're using 3-5 tools deeply. They know the shortcuts. They know the quirks. They've built muscle memory. That's the difference between a tourist and a local.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Here's how to pick tools that actually serve your work:</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The 3-Question Filter:</strong> Before adding any AI tool to your stack, ask: (1) Does this solve a real bottleneck in my process? (2) Can I learn it in under an hour? (3) Will I use it at least weekly? If you can't answer yes to all three, skip it.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="prompt-grid" style="margin-top:1.5rem">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>Writers</h4>
      <p>Your bottlenecks: blank page syndrome, editing fatigue, research rabbit holes. Look for tools that help with <strong>ideation, drafts, and line editing</strong> -- not tools that write for you.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>Visual Artists & Designers</h4>
      <p>Your bottlenecks: iteration speed, mockup volume, asset generation. Look for tools that help with <strong>concept exploration, background removal, and rapid prototyping</strong>.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>Musicians & Audio Creators</h4>
      <p>Your bottlenecks: arrangement experiments, mixing reference, sample creation. Look for tools that help with <strong>stem separation, melody sketching, and mastering assistance</strong>.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>Content Creators & Marketers</h4>
      <p>Your bottlenecks: repurposing content across platforms, thumbnail creation, caption writing. Look for tools that help with <strong>batch creation, format adaptation, and scheduling</strong>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2: FREE VS PAID -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Honest Comparison</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Free vs. paid: what you're actually getting.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Let's cut through the noise. Free tools are genuinely useful -- but they come with trade-offs that matter for professional creative work. Here's what's really going on.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>Free Tier Reality</h4>
      <ul style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.8;padding-left:1rem">
        <li>Lower resolution outputs (images, audio)</li>
        <li>Usage caps that hit mid-project</li>
        <li>Your work may train their models</li>
        <li>Watermarks on visual outputs</li>
        <li>Slower processing, queue-based</li>
        <li>Limited style control and customization</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>Paid Tier Reality</h4>
      <ul style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.8;padding-left:1rem">
        <li>Full resolution, commercial-ready output</li>
        <li>Higher or unlimited usage</li>
        <li>Your data stays private (usually)</li>
        <li>No watermarks, clean exports</li>
        <li>Priority processing</li>
        <li>Advanced controls, fine-tuning, API access</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Sophia's Take</div>
    <p>Use free tools to explore and learn. Use paid tools to produce and deliver. If a tool saves you more than 2 hours a month and you bill for your time, the paid version pays for itself. Do the math on YOUR hourly rate -- the answer usually surprises people.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 3: MULTI-TOOL WORKFLOW -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The System</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Setting up a workflow that actually flows.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The magic isn't in any single tool. It's in how tools <strong>talk to each other</strong>. A great creative AI workflow looks like an assembly line -- each station does one thing well, and the work moves smoothly from one to the next.</p>

  <p class="section-text">Here's a real example of a multi-tool content workflow:</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>Example: Blog Post Pipeline</strong></p>
    <p style="margin-top:.75rem;font-size:.9rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.8">
      <strong>Step 1 (Claude):</strong> Brainstorm angles, outline structure, draft the post.<br>
      <strong>Step 2 (Grammarly / ProWritingAid):</strong> Line-edit for clarity and tone.<br>
      <strong>Step 3 (Midjourney / DALL-E):</strong> Generate a featured image that matches the mood.<br>
      <strong>Step 4 (Canva AI):</strong> Resize that image into social media cards for every platform.<br>
      <strong>Step 5 (Claude again):</strong> Write platform-specific captions from the blog content.
    </p>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text">Notice how no single tool does everything. Each one has a job. The creative (you) is the director -- you make the taste decisions, the editorial calls, the "this feels right" judgments. The tools handle the labor.</p>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Workflow Design Principles</div>
    <ul style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.8;padding-left:1rem">
      <li><strong>One tool per bottleneck.</strong> Don't use three writing tools. Pick one and go deep.</li>
      <li><strong>Export formats matter.</strong> Make sure Tool A's output can be Tool B's input.</li>
      <li><strong>Build templates, not one-offs.</strong> If you do it twice, templatize it.</li>
      <li><strong>Document your workflow.</strong> Write down the steps. Future you will thank present you.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: YOUR PERSONAL STACK -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Your Stack</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The creative AI stack -- by discipline.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Here are battle-tested starting stacks for different creative disciplines. These aren't the only options -- they're starting points that work well together. Adapt to your taste.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>The Writer's Stack</h4>
      <p style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.8">
        <strong>Claude Pro</strong> -- ideation, drafts, research, editing partner<br>
        <strong>ProWritingAid</strong> -- style analysis, readability scoring<br>
        <strong>Otter.ai</strong> -- transcribe interviews and voice notes<br>
        <strong>Notion AI</strong> -- organize and resurface notes
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>The Designer's Stack</h4>
      <p style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.8">
        <strong>Midjourney / DALL-E</strong> -- concept art, mood boards<br>
        <strong>Adobe Firefly</strong> -- production-safe image generation<br>
        <strong>Remove.bg</strong> -- instant background removal<br>
        <strong>Figma AI</strong> -- layout suggestions, auto-resize
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>The Musician's Stack</h4>
      <p style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.8">
        <strong>Udio / Suno</strong> -- melody and arrangement sketching<br>
        <strong>LANDR</strong> -- AI mastering and distribution<br>
        <strong>Moises</strong> -- stem separation, practice tools<br>
        <strong>Claude</strong> -- lyrics, press materials, project planning
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <h4>The Content Creator's Stack</h4>
      <p style="font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim);line-height:1.8">
        <strong>Claude</strong> -- scripts, captions, content strategy<br>
        <strong>Canva AI</strong> -- thumbnails, social graphics<br>
        <strong>Descript</strong> -- AI video/podcast editing<br>
        <strong>Opus Clip</strong> -- auto-clip long videos into shorts
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>Start with two tools.</strong> Seriously. Pick the one that handles your biggest bottleneck and the one that handles your second-biggest. Master those. Add a third tool only when the first two feel effortless. Tool overload is the number one reason creatives give up on AI.</p>
  </div>
</div>
