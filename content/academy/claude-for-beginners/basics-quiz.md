<!-- THE PROMPT FORMULA RECAP -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Prompt Formula</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Role + Context + Format — the formula that works every time.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">This is the single most important skill from Module 1. If you remember nothing else, remember this: great prompts have three parts.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.75rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:16px">
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:#fb923c;font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0;margin-top:2px">R</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.95rem;margin-bottom:2px">Role — tell Claude who to be</div>
          <div style="color:#a1a1aa;font-size:.85rem">"You are an experienced project manager." "Act as a professional editor." "You are a marketing strategist." This sets the expertise level and perspective for the response.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(139,92,246,.12);color:#8b5cf6;font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0;margin-top:2px">C</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.95rem;margin-bottom:2px">Context — give Claude the situation</div>
          <div style="color:#a1a1aa;font-size:.85rem">Background information, constraints, who the audience is, what you have tried already. The more context you give, the less Claude has to guess — and the better the result.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(52,211,153,.12);color:#34d399;font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0;margin-top:2px">F</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.95rem;margin-bottom:2px">Format — tell Claude what you want back</div>
          <div style="color:#a1a1aa;font-size:.85rem">"Give me 5 bullet points." "Write a 3-paragraph email." "Create a comparison table." "Keep it under 100 words." Specifying format means less editing for you.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>Why this matters:</strong> A vague prompt like "Help me with marketing" forces Claude to guess everything. A specific prompt like "You are a marketing strategist. I run a small bakery with a $200/month budget. Give me 5 social media post ideas with captions" gives Claude everything it needs to nail the response.</p>
  </div>

<!-- COMMON MISTAKES -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Watch Out</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Mistakes beginners make — avoid these on the quiz.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">These are the most common misconceptions from Module 1. The quiz will test whether you have moved past them.</p>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Mistake #1: Being too vague</div>
    <p><strong>"Write me an email"</strong> is like walking into a restaurant and saying "give me food." You will get something, but it probably will not be what you wanted. Add role, context, and format to every prompt.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Mistake #2: Trusting facts blindly</div>
    <p><strong>Claude is excellent at reasoning and writing</strong>, but can occasionally get specific facts wrong. Always verify important claims — especially numbers, dates, and statistics — before using them in presentations or decisions.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Mistake #3: Thinking Claude replaces you</div>
    <p><strong>Claude is a tool, not a replacement.</strong> It makes you faster and better at your job. The winning combination is your judgment and experience plus Claude's speed and capabilities. You are still in charge.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Mistake #4: Thinking you need to be technical</div>
    <p><strong>You do not need to know how to code.</strong> You do not need to be a "prompt engineer." You just need to tell Claude three things: who to be, what the situation is, and what format you want. That is literally it.</p>
  </div>
</div>
