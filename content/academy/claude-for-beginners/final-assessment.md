<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Warm-Up</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Quick warm-up before the assessment.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Before you start the final 12 questions, here is a rapid-fire review of the most commonly missed concepts. Read through these carefully — they cover the trickiest areas.</p>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Common Mistake #1</div>
    <p><strong>Confusing Claude with a search engine.</strong> Claude is an AI assistant that reads, writes, and analyzes. It can search the web, but that is just one of many capabilities. The key difference: Claude gives you <em>answers and drafts</em>, not links.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Common Mistake #2</div>
    <p><strong>Thinking custom instructions are optional.</strong> They are not. Custom instructions are the single highest-impact setup step you can take. Five minutes of setup saves hours of repeating yourself in every conversation.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Common Mistake #3</div>
    <p><strong>Forgetting that each new conversation starts fresh.</strong> Within a single conversation, Claude remembers everything. But when you open a new conversation, context does not carry over automatically. Use custom instructions, memory, and projects to persist important context.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Common Mistake #4</div>
    <p><strong>Asking Claude to "just make it shorter" when summarizing.</strong> That gives Claude no direction on what to prioritize. Instead, specify a summary style: executive summary, action items, bullet points, or ELI5. Each one produces focused, useful output.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Common Mistake #5</div>
    <p><strong>Skipping tone instructions on emails.</strong> Tone is the difference between an email that sounds like you and one that sounds like a robot. Always name the tone: "professional but warm," "direct but kind," "casual but competent."</p>
  </div>
</div>
