<!-- SECTION 1: THE REALITY CHECK -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Reality Check</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Most "industry AI" stories are aspirational. Here's what's actually shipping.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Conference keynotes love to showcase AI moonshots. A hospital that uses AI to cure rare diseases. A bank that predicts market crashes. These stories are real, but they represent the top 1% of AI implementations backed by billions in R&D.</p>
  <p class="section-text">This lesson focuses on what <strong>mid-market and enterprise organizations are actually deploying today</strong> with existing budgets and existing teams. The boring, profitable stuff that doesn't make headlines but does make quarterly numbers.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2: HEALTHCARE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Healthcare</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Healthcare: where AI saves time AND lives.</h2>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Clinical documentation</strong>
      <p>AI scribes (Nuance DAX, Abridge) reduce physician documentation time by 50-70%. Doctors dictate naturally; AI generates structured notes. This is the highest-adoption AI use case in healthcare today.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Prior authorization</strong>
      <p>AI automates the back-and-forth with payers, reducing authorization time from days to hours. Companies like Olive AI and Infinitus handle millions of calls annually.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Medical imaging triage</strong>
      <p>AI flags critical findings in radiology scans (stroke, PE, fractures) and moves them to the front of the reading queue. Not replacing radiologists. Making them faster on the cases that matter most.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Still hype: Autonomous diagnosis</strong>
      <p>AI as a standalone diagnostician is years away from broad deployment. Regulatory, liability, and accuracy barriers remain significant. The FDA has approved narrow diagnostic AI tools, but they assist clinicians rather than replace them.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <strong>Regulatory reality:</strong> FDA approval for clinical AI takes 6-18 months. HIPAA compliance is table stakes. Any vendor without a BAA (Business Associate Agreement) is a non-starter.
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 3: FINANCE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Finance</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Finance: AI thrives where data is structured and stakes are high.</h2>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Fraud detection</strong>
      <p>Real-time transaction monitoring using ML catches fraud that rule-based systems miss. JPMorgan's COiN platform reviews 12,000 commercial credit agreements in seconds. Every major bank runs AI fraud models in production.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Document processing</strong>
      <p>Loan applications, compliance reports, regulatory filings. AI extracts, classifies, and validates data from unstructured documents 10x faster than manual review with 95%+ accuracy.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Customer service</strong>
      <p>AI handles 40-60% of routine banking inquiries without human intervention. Balance checks, transaction disputes, account changes. The ROI is immediate and measurable.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Still hype: Autonomous trading</strong>
      <p>AI-assisted trading analysis is real. Fully autonomous AI trading that consistently beats the market is not. The firms that claim otherwise are selling you something.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <strong>Regulatory reality:</strong> SEC and OCC guidance on AI is evolving rapidly. Model risk management (SR 11-7) applies to AI models. Explainability isn't optional in financial services. Document every model decision.
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: RETAIL -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Retail</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Retail: personalization at scale is the killer app.</h2>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Product recommendations</strong>
      <p>Amazon attributes 35% of revenue to its recommendation engine. You don't need Amazon's budget. Tools like Dynamic Yield and Bloomreach bring AI-powered recommendations to mid-market retailers for $2-10K/month.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Demand forecasting</strong>
      <p>AI reduces overstock by 20-30% and stockouts by 30-50%. It factors in weather, events, social trends, and historical data. The ROI hits directly on inventory carrying costs.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Dynamic pricing</strong>
      <p>Real-time price optimization based on demand, competition, and inventory levels. Airlines and hotels have done this for decades. Now AI makes it accessible to any retailer with an e-commerce presence.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Still hype: Fully automated stores</strong>
      <p>Amazon pulled back on Just Walk Out technology. Cashierless stores work in small formats but haven't scaled economically for most retailers. Self-checkout with AI assist is the realistic near-term play.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 5: MANUFACTURING -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Manufacturing</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Manufacturing: AI finds value in the physical world.</h2>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Predictive maintenance</strong>
      <p>Sensors + AI predict equipment failures before they happen. Typical results: 30-50% reduction in unplanned downtime, 10-20% reduction in maintenance costs. This is the most proven AI use case in manufacturing.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Visual quality inspection</strong>
      <p>Computer vision catches defects human inspectors miss. Runs 24/7 without fatigue. Accuracy rates of 98%+ on trained defect types. ROI is typically under 12 months.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Supply chain optimization</strong>
      <p>AI models optimize routing, supplier selection, and inventory positioning across complex supply networks. Post-pandemic, this moved from "nice to have" to strategic imperative.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Still hype: Lights-out factories</strong>
      <p>Fully autonomous manufacturing is decades away for complex products. AI-assisted operations with human oversight is the profitable reality. The factories winning with AI still have people in them.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 6: PROFESSIONAL SERVICES -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Professional Services</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Professional services: AI multiplies your highest-cost asset.</h2>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Document review (Legal)</strong>
      <p>AI reviews contracts, discovery documents, and regulatory filings 60-80% faster than associates. Harvey, CoCounsel, and similar tools are in production at major law firms. The economics are transformative for high-volume legal work.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Audit automation (Accounting)</strong>
      <p>AI flags anomalies in financial data, automates sampling, and generates preliminary audit findings. Big Four firms all have proprietary AI audit tools in production.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Working now: Research synthesis (Consulting)</strong>
      <p>AI processes hundreds of sources to generate market landscapes, competitive analyses, and trend reports. Junior consultants spend less time gathering and more time analyzing.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Still hype: AI replacing partners</strong>
      <p>Client relationships, strategic judgment, and rainmaking remain deeply human. AI makes professionals more productive, which means firms can serve more clients at higher margins. The winners use AI to do more, not to employ fewer people.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The pattern across every industry:</strong> AI delivers the fastest ROI on high-volume, data-rich, repetitive tasks. It augments human judgment rather than replacing it. The organizations winning with AI started with their most painful bottleneck, proved value in 90 days, and expanded from there.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">

  <p class="section-text">The good news: starting now with proven use cases lets you leapfrog the pioneers' early mistakes. You don't need to be first. You need to be fast and focused.</p>
