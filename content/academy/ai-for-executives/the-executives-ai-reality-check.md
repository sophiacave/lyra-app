<!-- SECTION 1: THE STATE OF AI -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Landscape</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">What AI actually does in 2026.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">You've sat through the vendor pitches. You've read the McKinsey reports. You've heard "AI will transform everything" so many times it's lost all meaning. So let's start with what's real.</p>
  <p class="section-text">AI in 2026 is <strong>extraordinarily good at a narrow set of things</strong> and mediocre-to-dangerous at everything else. The executives who win aren't the ones adopting the most AI. They're the ones deploying it where it actually works.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">&#9989;</div>
      <h4>AI Is Strong Here</h4>
      <p><strong>Language tasks:</strong> drafting, summarizing, translating, analyzing text at scale. <strong>Pattern recognition:</strong> finding anomalies in data, forecasting from historical patterns. <strong>Automation:</strong> routing, classification, repetitive decision-making with clear rules.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">&#9888;&#65039;</div>
      <h4>AI Is Maturing Here</h4>
      <p><strong>Complex reasoning:</strong> multi-step analysis is improving but still needs human oversight. <strong>Creative work:</strong> useful for drafts and ideation, not for final output. <strong>Code generation:</strong> accelerates developers significantly, but doesn't replace engineering judgment.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">&#10060;</div>
      <h4>AI Is Unreliable Here</h4>
      <p><strong>Novel strategy:</strong> AI recombines existing patterns, it doesn't create new ones. <strong>Relationship judgment:</strong> negotiations, culture reads, stakeholder management. <strong>High-stakes decisions:</strong> anything where being confidently wrong is catastrophic.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The executive filter:</strong> If a vendor can't tell you specifically where AI fails in their product, they're selling you hype. Every honest AI company knows its limitations. The ones that don't disclose them aren't being honest.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2: VALUE VS HYPE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Filter</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Separating value from vapor.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">There are roughly $200 billion worth of AI investments in play across the enterprise market right now. History tells us that about 30% of those will generate meaningful returns. The rest will end up as expensive lessons.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The difference? <strong>Value comes from solving a specific, measurable business problem.</strong> Vapor comes from "implementing AI" as a goal in itself. Here's how to tell the difference in under 60 seconds:</p>

<!-- SECTION 3: THE THREE QUESTIONS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Framework</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">3 questions before you greenlight anything.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Before any AI initiative gets budget, headcount, or executive attention, it needs to pass through these three gates. Write them on a card. Tape them to your monitor. Use them in every meeting where someone pitches an AI project.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">1&#65039;&#8419;</div>
      <h4>"What's the business problem?"</h4>
      <p>Not "what can AI do for us" but "what problem are we solving, and would we invest in solving it even if AI didn't exist?" If the answer is no, the project isn't worth your time regardless of the technology.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">2&#65039;&#8419;</div>
      <h4>"Where's the data?"</h4>
      <p>AI runs on data. If your data is scattered across 15 systems, unstructured, ungoverned, or incomplete, the AI project will fail before it starts. The honest answer here saves you months of expensive discovery later.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <div class="emoji">3&#65039;&#8419;</div>
      <h4>"What does 'good enough' look like?"</h4>
      <p>AI is probabilistic. It gives you 85-95% accuracy on most tasks, not 100%. If your use case can't tolerate that margin of error, AI isn't the right tool. If it can, define the acceptable threshold before you start.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <div class="tip-label">Executive Prompt Template</div>
    <p>Use this in your next AI briefing: <em>"Walk me through the specific workflow this changes. Show me the current cost of that workflow, the expected cost after AI, and how we'll measure the difference at 30, 60, and 90 days."</em> Any team that can't answer this isn't ready to deploy.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: WHERE PEERS SEE RETURNS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Evidence</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Where real executives are seeing real returns.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Across industries, the highest-ROI AI deployments share a pattern: they target <strong>high-volume, repetitive, language-heavy processes</strong> where speed matters and perfection doesn't. Here's where the returns are proven, not projected:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="padding:.75rem 1rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;color:var(--text)">Customer Support Triage</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem;margin-top:4px">AI handles 40-60% of tier-1 inquiries, routing complex issues to humans with full context. Typical result: 35% cost reduction, higher customer satisfaction because response times drop from hours to seconds.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem 1rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;color:var(--text)">Document Processing & Compliance</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem;margin-top:4px">Contract review, regulatory filing analysis, policy comparison. AI reads in seconds what took analysts hours. Typical result: 70% faster turnaround, fewer missed clauses than manual review.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem 1rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;color:var(--text)">Internal Knowledge Management</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem;margin-top:4px">Employees ask an AI that's been trained on internal docs instead of searching 14 SharePoint sites. Typical result: 25% reduction in time-to-answer, dramatically less institutional knowledge loss during turnover.</div>
      </div>
      <div style="padding:.75rem 1rem;background:var(--bg);border:1px solid var(--border);border-radius:10px">
        <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.9rem;color:var(--text)">Sales Enablement & Pipeline Analysis</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.8rem;margin-top:4px">AI summarizes call transcripts, drafts follow-ups, scores pipeline health, and surfaces deals at risk. Typical result: 15-20% improvement in win rates, reps spend 30% more time selling vs. admin.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>Notice the pattern:</strong> none of these are moonshot projects. They're taking existing, well-understood processes and making them faster and cheaper. That's where AI earns its keep in 2026. The moonshots come later, built on the foundation of these proven wins.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- KEY TAKEAWAY -->
<div class="callout purple">
  <p><strong>The executive takeaway:</strong> AI is a powerful operational tool, not a magic strategy generator. The leaders who benefit most are the ones who start with a clear business problem, honest data assessment, and realistic expectations about accuracy. That discipline separates the 30% who see returns from the 70% who don't.</p>
</div>
