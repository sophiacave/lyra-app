<!-- SECTION 1: THE VENDOR LANDSCAPE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Problem</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The AI vendor market is a minefield.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">There are now over 14,000 AI startups competing for your budget. Most of them are wrappers around the same foundation models you could access directly. Some are genuinely transformative. The difference between a $200K mistake and a 10x productivity gain often comes down to <strong>how you evaluate before you buy</strong>.</p>
  <p class="section-text">The playbook below is battle-tested. It comes from watching organizations make every possible vendor mistake and distilling the patterns into a repeatable process.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2: RED FLAGS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Red Flags</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">7 red flags that should end a conversation.</h2>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>1. "Our AI is proprietary"</strong>
      <p>If they won't tell you what model they're built on, they're hiding something. Most enterprise AI tools use OpenAI, Anthropic, or open-source models underneath. That's fine. Lying about it isn't.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>2. No data retention policy</strong>
      <p>Ask: "What happens to our data after we cancel?" If the answer is vague, walk away. Your data should be exportable, deletable, and never used for training without explicit consent.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>3. ROI claims without methodology</strong>
      <p>"Our customers see 400% ROI" means nothing without knowing who, how, and over what timeframe. Demand case studies with named companies and verifiable metrics.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>4. No pilot or proof-of-concept option</strong>
      <p>Any vendor confident in their product will let you test it on real data for 30-90 days. Annual contracts with no trial period are a red flag.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>5. Per-seat pricing that scales exponentially</strong>
      <p>AI costs are dropping every quarter. If a vendor's pricing model gets dramatically more expensive as you scale, their economics don't work. You'll be paying for their inefficiency.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>6. "We replace your team"</strong>
      <p>No responsible AI vendor makes this claim. AI augments teams. Vendors who promise to eliminate headcount are selling a fantasy and setting you up for implementation failure.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>7. No SOC 2 or equivalent compliance</strong>
      <p>If they're handling your enterprise data without SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, or equivalent certification, they're not ready for enterprise customers. Full stop.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 3: DEMO CHECKLIST -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Demo</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Your AI demo checklist.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Bring this to every vendor meeting. Score each item 1-5. Any vendor scoring below 30/50 doesn't make the shortlist.</p>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <strong>Before the demo</strong> — Send the vendor YOUR data (anonymized if needed) and ask them to demo with it. Canned demos with perfect data prove nothing. How the tool handles your messy, real-world data is the only thing that matters.
  </div>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Integration</strong><p>Does it connect to your existing stack? CRM, ERP, data warehouse? Native integrations beat "we have an API" every time.</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Time to value</strong><p>How long from contract signing to first measurable result? Anything over 90 days for a SaaS tool is a warning sign.</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Accuracy on your data</strong><p>Run their tool on 100 real examples. Measure accuracy yourself. Don't trust their benchmarks.</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Explainability</strong><p>Can you see WHY the AI made a decision? Black-box AI is unacceptable in regulated industries and risky everywhere else.</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Failure handling</strong><p>What happens when the AI gets it wrong? Is there a human escalation path? How does the system learn from mistakes?</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Admin controls</strong><p>Can you set guardrails, restrict data access, manage permissions? Enterprise AI without governance features isn't enterprise-ready.</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Support model</strong><p>Who helps when things break? Dedicated CSM? 24/7 support? Community forum? Match the support level to your team's technical maturity.</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Roadmap transparency</strong><p>Where is the product going? How do they handle model upgrades? A vendor that won't share their roadmap doesn't trust you as a partner.</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Exit strategy</strong><p>How easy is it to leave? Data portability, contract flexibility, migration support. The best vendors make it easy to leave because they know you won't.</p></div>
    <div class="prompt-card"><strong>Reference customers</strong><p>Talk to at least two current customers in your industry. Ask what surprised them. Ask what almost made them cancel.</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: CONTRACT NEGOTIATION -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Contract</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Negotiate like the AI market is in your favor. Because it is.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">There are more AI vendors than enterprise customers. That gives you leverage. Use it.</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>Five non-negotiables for every AI contract:</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Performance SLAs</strong> — Define minimum accuracy, uptime, and response time. Tie them to credits or termination rights.</li>
      <li><strong>Data ownership clause</strong> — Your data stays yours. Period. No training on your data without written consent. Full deletion on termination.</li>
      <li><strong>Price protection</strong> — Cap annual increases at 5-8%. AI costs are dropping; your vendor shouldn't be raising prices 20% annually.</li>
      <li><strong>Quarterly exit option</strong> — Push for 90-day termination for convenience after the first year. If they won't budge, demand a 30-day termination for cause with clearly defined triggers.</li>
      <li><strong>Model change notification</strong> — If the vendor switches the underlying AI model, you get 60 days notice and the right to test before the change goes live.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 5: BUILD VS BUY -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Decision</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The build-vs-buy decision matrix.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">This isn't about technical preference. It's about where AI creates competitive advantage for your organization versus where it's a utility.</p>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <strong>The rule of thumb:</strong> If AI is touching your core differentiator, build. If it's optimizing a back-office process that every company has, buy. If you're unsure, buy first, learn from it, then build what matters.
  </div>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Buy when...</strong>
      <p>The problem is common across industries. Time to value matters more than customization. Your team lacks ML engineering talent. The vendor has domain expertise you don't.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Build when...</strong>
      <p>The AI IS your product or core differentiator. You have proprietary data that creates a moat. You need full control over model behavior. Regulatory requirements demand it.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Partner when...</strong>
      <p>You need custom but can't hire fast enough. The problem requires domain expertise AND AI expertise. You want to build internal capability over time with expert guidance.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Wait when...</strong>
      <p>The use case is experimental with no clear ROI. The technology isn't mature enough for your requirements. Your data infrastructure isn't ready. Regulation is still evolving.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 6: TAKEAWAY -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Bottom Line</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Process beats intuition in vendor selection.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The executives who get burned by AI vendors are the ones who skip the process. They fall in love with a demo, sign a two-year contract, and discover the limitations six months in. The playbook above isn't complicated. It's disciplined. Use it every time, and you'll make better bets than 90% of the market.</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>Your next step:</strong> Download the demo checklist, customize it for your industry, and bring it to your next vendor meeting. Score ruthlessly. Trust the numbers, not the pitch.</p>
  </div>
