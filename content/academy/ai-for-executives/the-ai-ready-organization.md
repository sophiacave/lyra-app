<!-- SECTION 1: WHY READINESS MATTERS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Foundation</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">87% of AI projects never make it to production. Readiness is why.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The AI tools work. The models are good enough. The ROI is real. So why do most enterprise AI initiatives stall or fail? Because organizations skip the readiness work. They buy the tool before preparing the data. They launch the pilot before getting buy-in. They hire the engineer before defining the problem.</p>
  <p class="section-text">AI readiness isn't glamorous. It's the plumbing, wiring, and foundation that makes everything else possible. Get it right, and AI adoption accelerates. Skip it, and you'll spend twice the time and budget fixing problems that were preventable.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2: DATA READINESS -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Pillar 1</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Data readiness: your AI is only as good as your data.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI doesn't create intelligence from nothing. It finds patterns in data. If your data is scattered across 15 systems, full of duplicates, and missing key fields, your AI outputs will reflect that chaos perfectly.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Accessibility</strong>
      <p>Can you pull data from all relevant systems into one place within hours, not weeks? If your data lives in silos that don't talk to each other, that's your first AI blocker.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Quality</strong>
      <p>Are records complete, consistent, and accurate? Run a simple test: pull 100 random customer records. How many have missing fields, outdated info, or duplicate entries? That percentage is your data quality score.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Volume</strong>
      <p>Do you have enough data for AI to find meaningful patterns? For most business applications, you need thousands of examples, not millions. But you need them to be clean and labeled.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Governance</strong>
      <p>Who owns each data set? Who can access it? How long is it retained? If you can't answer these questions, you're not ready for AI that touches sensitive data.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <strong>The fastest path to data readiness:</strong> Pick your top AI use case. Identify the 2-3 data sources it requires. Clean and connect those specific sources. Don't try to fix all your data at once. Fix the data that feeds your first AI win.
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 3: INFRASTRUCTURE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Pillar 2</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Infrastructure: you probably have more than you think.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI infrastructure in 2026 is not what it was in 2020. You don't need GPU clusters and a Kubernetes platform. For 80% of enterprise AI use cases, you need a modern cloud stack, clean APIs, and SaaS tools that handle the compute for you.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Cloud foundation</strong>
      <p>Are you on AWS, Azure, or GCP? If yes, you already have access to AI services. If you're still running critical workloads on-premise with no cloud strategy, that's a prerequisite.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>API connectivity</strong>
      <p>Can your core systems (CRM, ERP, HRIS) talk to external services via API? Modern AI is built on API integration. Legacy systems without APIs need middleware or replacement.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Identity and access</strong>
      <p>Do you have SSO, role-based access control, and audit logging? AI tools need the same security infrastructure as any enterprise SaaS. No exceptions.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Data pipeline</strong>
      <p>Can you move data from source systems to a central location automatically? Tools like Fivetran, Airbyte, or built-in cloud ETL make this accessible without a large data engineering team.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: CULTURE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Pillar 3</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Culture change: the hardest part of AI adoption.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">You can have perfect data, modern infrastructure, and the best AI tools money can buy. If your people don't trust AI, won't use it, or actively resist it, none of that matters.</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>The three fears driving AI resistance:</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>"AI will take my job"</strong> — Address this directly. Show how AI changes roles rather than eliminates them. Highlight real examples of people in your industry who became more valuable by using AI.</li>
      <li><strong>"I'll look stupid trying to use it"</strong> — Make AI experimentation psychologically safe. Celebrate failures and learning. Never penalize someone for trying AI and getting a bad result.</li>
      <li><strong>"I can't trust AI output"</strong> — This is healthy skepticism. Channel it into a verification culture. Human review of AI output isn't a weakness; it's a feature. Build it into every workflow.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Leadership modeling</strong>
      <p>When executives visibly use AI and talk about what they've learned, adoption cascades. When AI is a "technology initiative" that leadership delegates entirely, it stalls. Use it yourself. Talk about it. Show what's possible.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Quick wins first</strong>
      <p>Start with use cases that are obviously painful and where AI obviously helps. Email drafting. Meeting summarization. Report generation. When people experience the value personally, resistance melts.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Training as investment</strong>
      <p>Budget 40+ hours of AI training per employee in Year 1. Not a lunch-and-learn. Structured, hands-on training with their actual work. People who feel competent with AI become advocates for AI.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Incentive alignment</strong>
      <p>If your performance reviews don't mention AI adoption, your culture message is clear: AI is optional. Add AI experimentation and efficiency to goals and reviews across the organization.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 5: THE MATURITY MODEL -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Model</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The 5-stage AI maturity model.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Where is your organization today? Be honest. The goal isn't to be at Stage 5 immediately. The goal is to know where you are and move deliberately to the next stage.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Stage 1: AI-Curious</strong>
      <p>Leadership is interested. No formal strategy. Individual employees experimenting on their own. No governance. No budget. This is where most organizations were in early 2024.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Stage 2: AI-Exploring</strong>
      <p>First pilot projects underway. Small budget allocated. An informal AI champion exists. Basic usage policies in place. Data quality issues are being discovered. Early wins generating excitement.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Stage 3: AI-Implementing</strong>
      <p>Multiple AI use cases in production. Dedicated team or roles. Formal AI strategy and governance. Measurable ROI being tracked. Data infrastructure improving. Training programs established.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Stage 4: AI-Integrated</strong>
      <p>AI is embedded in core business processes. Most employees use AI tools daily. Custom AI solutions built for competitive advantage. Strong data culture. AI governance is mature and efficient.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Stage 5: AI-Native</strong>
      <p>AI is inseparable from how the organization operates. Every process is AI-augmented by default. AI informs strategic decisions at the board level. Continuous AI innovation is part of the culture. This is the destination, not the starting point.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tip-box">
    <strong>Realistic timeline:</strong> Moving one stage takes 6-12 months with focused effort. Organizations that try to jump from Stage 1 to Stage 4 in a year almost always crash. The ones that move stage by stage, proving value at each level, are the ones that reach AI-Native.
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 6: SELF-ASSESSMENT -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Assessment</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Rate your organization on each pillar.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Score each area 1-5 (1 = not started, 5 = mature). Your lowest score is your biggest blocker. Fix that first.</p>

  <div class="prompt-grid">
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Data Readiness (1-5)</strong>
      <p>Can you access clean, structured data for your top 3 AI use cases within a week? Is there an owner for each critical data set? Do you have data quality metrics?</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Infrastructure (1-5)</strong>
      <p>Are you on modern cloud with API-connected systems? Do you have SSO, RBAC, and audit logging? Can you stand up a new AI tool in days, not months?</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Culture (1-5)</strong>
      <p>Do leaders model AI use? Is experimentation safe? Are employees trained? Is AI adoption in performance goals? Do people volunteer for AI projects?</p>
    </div>
    <div class="prompt-card">
      <strong>Governance (1-5)</strong>
      <p>Do you have an AI usage policy? Is there an ethics review process? Are vendor contracts AI-aware? Do you know where AI touches customer data?</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="callout">
    <p><strong>If your total score is under 8:</strong> You're AI-Curious (Stage 1). Focus entirely on data readiness and leadership buy-in for the next quarter.</p>
    <p><strong>If your total is 8-12:</strong> You're AI-Exploring (Stage 2). Formalize your AI strategy, assign an AI Champion, and launch your first governed pilot.</p>
    <p><strong>If your total is 13-16:</strong> You're AI-Implementing (Stage 3). Scale what's working, invest in training, and build your AI team.</p>
    <p><strong>If your total is 17-20:</strong> You're AI-Integrated or AI-Native. Focus on competitive differentiation and continuous innovation.</p>
  </div>
</div>
