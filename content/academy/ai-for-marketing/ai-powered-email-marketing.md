<!-- SECTION 1: SUBJECT LINES -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Subject Lines</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The subject line is 80% of the battle.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Your email lives or dies in the inbox. AI is perfect for this: generating dozens of subject line options in seconds so you can pick the best.</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem;background:var(--surface);border:1px solid var(--border2)">
    <pre style="font-family:'JetBrains Mono',monospace;font-size:.8rem;color:var(--text);line-height:1.8;white-space:pre-wrap;margin:0">Generate 15 email subject lines for:

<span style="color:var(--purple)">Product:</span> Online course about AI for small business
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Audience:</span> Small business owners, 30-55, non-technical
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Goal:</span> Get them to open and click to the sales page
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Tone:</span> Conversational, curious, not clickbait

<span style="color:var(--orange)">Give me 5 in each style:</span>
- Curiosity-driven (make them wonder)
- Benefit-driven (promise a clear outcome)
- Urgency-driven (time-sensitive, not spammy)

<span style="color:var(--red)">Avoid:</span> ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation!!!, "you won't believe," anything that triggers spam filters.</pre>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2: BODY COPY -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Body Copy</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Email body copy that sounds like a human wrote it.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">The #1 mistake with AI email copy: it sounds like AI. Generic, overpolished, and soulless. Here's the fix:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem;background:var(--surface);border:1px solid var(--border2)">
    <pre style="font-family:'JetBrains Mono',monospace;font-size:.8rem;color:var(--text);line-height:1.8;white-space:pre-wrap;margin:0">Write a marketing email.

<span style="color:var(--purple)">From:</span> [Your name], founder of [company]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">To:</span> Existing subscribers who haven't purchased yet
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Product:</span> [What you're selling + price]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Key benefit:</span> [The #1 thing they get]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Social proof:</span> [Testimonial, number of customers, result]

<span style="color:var(--orange)">Style rules:</span>
- Write like you're emailing ONE person, not a list
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max)
- One clear CTA — don't give multiple options
- Open with a story, observation, or question — NOT "I hope this email finds you well"
- Under 200 words

<span style="color:var(--red)">Don't:</span> Use "unlock," "game-changer," "revolutionary," or "don't miss out."
Sound like a human who genuinely wants to help, not a marketer who wants a click.</pre>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 3: SEQUENCES -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Sequences</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Build a 5-email welcome sequence in 20 minutes.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Email sequences are where AI really shines. Instead of writing each email individually, give AI the full arc and let it build the sequence:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem;background:var(--surface);border:1px solid var(--border2)">
    <pre style="font-family:'JetBrains Mono',monospace;font-size:.8rem;color:var(--text);line-height:1.8;white-space:pre-wrap;margin:0">Create a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers.

<span style="color:var(--purple)">Business:</span> [Your business, what you sell]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Subscriber source:</span> [How they signed up — lead magnet, blog, etc.]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Goal:</span> Build trust → educate → convert to first purchase

<span style="color:var(--orange)">Sequence arc:</span>
Email 1 (Day 0): Welcome + deliver the lead magnet + set expectations
Email 2 (Day 2): Your story — why you started this business
Email 3 (Day 4): Teach something valuable (no pitch)
Email 4 (Day 7): Case study or testimonial + soft pitch
Email 5 (Day 10): Direct offer with urgency

<span style="color:var(--blue)">For each email give me:</span>
- Subject line (2 options)
- Preview text
- Full body copy
- CTA button text

<span style="color:var(--red)">Voice:</span> [Your brand voice description]</pre>
  </div>

  <p class="section-text" style="margin-top:1rem">You'll need to edit and personalize each email, but AI gives you a working first draft in minutes instead of days.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: A/B TESTING -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">A/B Testing</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Use AI to generate test variants instantly.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI makes A/B testing trivially easy. Instead of agonizing over two subject lines, generate 10 and pick the best 2 to test:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">

        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Subject line tests:</strong> Generate 10 options, pick 2 that feel most different in approach</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(74,222,128,.12);color:var(--green);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">B</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>CTA tests:</strong> "Start free trial" vs "See it in action" vs "Get instant access" — generate 8, test 2</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(74,222,128,.12);color:var(--green);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">C</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Opening line tests:</strong> Story opener vs question opener vs stat opener — AI can write all three versions</div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(74,222,128,.12);color:var(--green);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">D</div>
        <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem"><strong>Length tests:</strong> Ask AI for a 50-word version and a 200-word version of the same email</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <p class="section-text" style="margin-top:1rem">The marketer who tests 10 variants per month will outperform the one who sends "whatever felt right" every single time.</p>
