<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Google Ads</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Google Search Ads: tight characters, big results.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Google Ads have strict character limits (30 chars per headline, 90 per description). AI excels at writing within tight constraints:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem;background:var(--surface);border:1px solid var(--border2)">
    <pre style="font-family:'JetBrains Mono',monospace;font-size:.8rem;color:var(--text);line-height:1.8;white-space:pre-wrap;margin:0">Write Google Search Ad copy for:

<span style="color:var(--purple)">Product:</span> [What you're advertising]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Target keyword:</span> [What someone searched]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Landing page offer:</span> [What they'll get when they click]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Unique selling point:</span> [Why you, not a competitor?]

<span style="color:var(--orange)">Generate 5 complete ads, each with:</span>
- Headline 1 (max 30 characters) — include the keyword
- Headline 2 (max 30 characters) — benefit or USP
- Headline 3 (max 30 characters) — CTA
- Description 1 (max 90 characters) — expand on the offer
- Description 2 (max 90 characters) — social proof or urgency

<span style="color:var(--red)">Strictly respect character limits.</span> Count every character.
Don't use all caps. Don't use excessive punctuation.</pre>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 2 -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Meta Ads</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Facebook & Instagram Ads: stop the scroll.</h2>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem;background:var(--surface);border:1px solid var(--border2)">
    <pre style="font-family:'JetBrains Mono',monospace;font-size:.8rem;color:var(--text);line-height:1.8;white-space:pre-wrap;margin:0">Write Facebook/Instagram ad copy for:

<span style="color:var(--purple)">Product:</span> [What you're selling]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Audience:</span> [Demographics + psychographics]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Pain point:</span> [The problem your product solves]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Offer:</span> [What they get — discount, free trial, etc.]

<span style="color:var(--orange)">Write 3 versions in different styles:</span>
1. Problem-agitate-solve (start with the pain)
2. Testimonial-style (start with a result/quote)
3. Direct offer (lead with the deal)

<span style="color:var(--blue)">For each version:</span>
- Primary text (first 3 lines MUST hook — that's all they see before "See more")
- Headline (under 40 characters)
- Description (under 30 characters)
- CTA button suggestion

<span style="color:var(--red)">Sound like a friend recommending something, not a brand selling something.</span></pre>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Psychology</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">5 psychological triggers AI can weave into your ads.</h2>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:12px">
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start;padding-bottom:12px;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">1</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--text)">Social Proof</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">"Join 10,000+ marketers" — tell AI to include specific numbers, testimonials, or trust signals.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start;padding-bottom:12px;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">2</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--text)">Scarcity</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">"Only 50 spots left" or "Ends Friday" — real scarcity, not manufactured urgency.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start;padding-bottom:12px;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">3</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--text)">Specificity</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">"Save 4.5 hours per week" beats "save time." Specific numbers feel real and credible.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start;padding-bottom:12px;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">4</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--text)">Loss Aversion</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">"Stop wasting $500/month on tools that don't work" — people fear loss more than they desire gain.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">
        <div style="background:rgba(251,146,60,.12);color:var(--orange);font-weight:800;font-size:.75rem;padding:4px 10px;border-radius:6px;flex-shrink:0">5</div>
        <div>
          <div style="font-weight:700;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--text)">Identity</div>
          <div style="color:var(--dim);font-size:.85rem">"For marketers who refuse to settle for average" — make the audience see themselves in the ad.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <p class="section-text" style="margin-top:1rem">Add these to your prompts: "Use social proof and specificity. Include a real number. Frame the benefit as avoiding a loss."</p>
