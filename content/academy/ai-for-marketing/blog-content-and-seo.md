<!-- SECTION 1: TOPIC IDEATION -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Topic Research</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Find topics your audience actually searches for.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Instead of guessing what to write about, use AI to generate topic clusters based on your business:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem;background:var(--surface);border:1px solid var(--border2)">
    <pre style="font-family:'JetBrains Mono',monospace;font-size:.8rem;color:var(--text);line-height:1.8;white-space:pre-wrap;margin:0">I run a <span style="color:var(--orange)">[type of business]</span> targeting <span style="color:var(--orange)">[audience]</span>.

Generate 20 blog post ideas organized into 4 topic clusters.
Each cluster should have a pillar post (comprehensive, 2000+ words)
and 4 supporting posts (focused, 800-1200 words) that link to it.

<span style="color:var(--purple)">For each post idea:</span>
- Suggested title (SEO-friendly, under 60 characters)
- Target keyword phrase
- Search intent (informational/commercial/navigational)
- Estimated difficulty (low/medium/high competition)

<span style="color:var(--red)">Focus on:</span> Topics where someone searching would be a potential customer.
Skip purely educational topics that attract the wrong audience.</pre>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Writing</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The blog post prompt that gets you 80% there.</h2>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem;background:var(--surface);border:1px solid var(--border2)">
    <pre style="font-family:'JetBrains Mono',monospace;font-size:.8rem;color:var(--text);line-height:1.8;white-space:pre-wrap;margin:0">Write a blog post:

<span style="color:var(--purple)">Title:</span> [Your SEO-optimized title]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Target keyword:</span> [Primary keyword phrase]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Word count:</span> [800-2000 depending on topic depth]
<span style="color:var(--purple)">Audience:</span> [Who's reading and what they know]

<span style="color:var(--orange)">Structure:</span>
- Hook opening (NOT "In today's digital age...")
- Clear H2 subheadings (include keyword variations naturally)
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Practical examples or actionable steps
- Conclusion with a clear next step or CTA

<span style="color:var(--blue)">SEO requirements:</span>
- Use the target keyword in the first 100 words
- Include 3-5 related keywords naturally throughout
- Write a meta description (under 155 characters)
- Suggest 3 internal linking opportunities

<span style="color:var(--red)">Voice:</span> [Your brand voice]
<span style="color:var(--red)">Don't:</span> Stuff keywords, write filler paragraphs, use "in conclusion," or start any paragraph with "It's important to note that."</pre>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 4: GOOGLE -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Google Question</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">How to avoid AI content penalties.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">Google's stance: they don't penalize AI content for being AI-generated. They penalize <strong>low-quality, unhelpful content</strong> — regardless of who or what wrote it. The rules are the same they've always been:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem">
    <div style="display:grid;grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr;gap:16px">
      <div>
        <div style="font-size:.7rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--green);text-transform:uppercase;letter-spacing:1px;margin-bottom:8px">Google Loves</div>
        <ul style="list-style:none;padding:0;margin:0;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim)">
          <li style="padding:6px 0;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">Original insights and expertise</li>
          <li style="padding:6px 0;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">Content that solves real problems</li>
          <li style="padding:6px 0;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">Unique data, examples, or perspectives</li>
          <li style="padding:6px 0">Content written for humans, not algorithms</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div>
        <div style="font-size:.7rem;font-weight:700;color:var(--red);text-transform:uppercase;letter-spacing:1px;margin-bottom:8px">Google Penalizes</div>
        <ul style="list-style:none;padding:0;margin:0;font-size:.85rem;color:var(--dim)">
          <li style="padding:6px 0;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">Thin content that says nothing new</li>
          <li style="padding:6px 0;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">Mass-produced content farms</li>
          <li style="padding:6px 0;border-bottom:1px solid var(--border)">Keyword stuffing and SEO manipulation</li>
          <li style="padding:6px 0">Content that exists only to rank, not to help</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <p class="section-text" style="margin-top:1rem"><strong>The formula:</strong> AI draft + your expertise + your examples + your editing = content that ranks AND helps people. Skip any of those steps and you're just adding noise to the internet.</p>
</div>

<!-- SECTION 6: KEYWORD STRATEGY -->
<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Keyword Strategy</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The AI keyword research workflow.</h2>
  <p class="section-text">You don't need expensive SEO tools to do keyword research. AI can help you build a keyword strategy from scratch:</p>

  <div class="demo-container" style="padding:1.5rem;background:var(--surface);border:1px solid var(--border2)">
    <pre style="font-family:'JetBrains Mono',monospace;font-size:.8rem;color:var(--text);line-height:1.8;white-space:pre-wrap;margin:0">I run a <span style="color:#fb923c">[type of business]</span> targeting <span style="color:#fb923c">[audience]</span>.

Help me build a keyword strategy:

<span style="color:#8b5cf6">Step 1:</span> Generate 10 "seed keywords" — broad terms
my audience would search for.

<span style="color:#8b5cf6">Step 2:</span> For each seed keyword, generate 5 long-tail
variations (3-5 word phrases with lower competition).

<span style="color:#8b5cf6">Step 3:</span> Categorize all 60 keywords by search intent:
- Informational ("how to...", "what is...")
- Commercial ("best...", "vs...", "review...")
- Transactional ("buy...", "pricing...", "sign up...")

<span style="color:#34d399">Then prioritize:</span> Which 10 keywords should I target
first? Consider: relevance to my business, likely
competition level, and closeness to a purchase decision.

<span style="color:#ef4444">Format as a table:</span> Keyword | Intent | Priority | Blog Post Idea</pre>
  </div>
  <p class="section-text" style="margin-top:1rem"><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Target commercial and transactional keywords first — those are people closer to buying. Informational keywords build traffic but convert less directly. A mix of both is ideal, but revenue-driving keywords come first.</p>
</div>
