<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">The Revolution</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">Music Creation Without Music Theory</h2>
  <p class="section-text">AI music generation is the most mind-bending part of the audio revolution. You type words. Music comes out. Not random noise — actual compositions with structure, melody, harmony, and feeling. It shouldn't work. It does.</p>
  <p class="section-text">Models like Suno and Udio were trained on massive datasets of music. They learned what makes a blues riff feel like longing, what makes a four-on-the-floor beat make you move, what makes a minor key progression tug at your chest. They don't understand music the way a musician does. But they can produce it in ways that genuinely move people.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Tools</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The AI Music Toolkit</h2>
  <p class="section-text"><strong style="color: var(--orange);">Suno:</strong> The most popular AI music generator. Text-to-song with lyrics, instrumentation, and production. V4 produces remarkably polished tracks. Free tier gives you 50 credits per day. Commercial license on paid plans.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong style="color: var(--purple);">Udio:</strong> Suno's main competitor. Some users prefer its audio fidelity, especially for complex arrangements. Better at capturing specific production styles. Worth testing both for any project.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong style="color: var(--green);">Stable Audio:</strong> Stability AI's music model. Strong for sound effects, ambient textures, and shorter clips. Good API for integration into apps and workflows.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong style="color: var(--blue);">AIVA:</strong> Designed for film and game scoring. More structured composition approach. Gives you MIDI output so you can edit arrangements in a traditional DAW. Great for when you need more control.</p>
  <p class="section-text"><strong style="color: var(--red);">Mubert:</strong> Generates endless streams of royalty-free background music. Perfect for videos, streams, and ambient content. API lets you generate music programmatically.</p>
</div>

<div class="lesson-section">
  <span class="section-label">Craft</span>
  <h2 class="section-title">The Art of the Music Prompt</h2>
  <p class="section-text">A good music prompt has four components: <strong>genre</strong> (lo-fi hip hop, orchestral, punk rock), <strong>mood</strong> (melancholic, triumphant, uneasy), <strong>instrumentation</strong> (acoustic guitar, synthesizer, strings), and <strong>production style</strong> (warm analog, crisp modern, lo-fi tape hiss).</p>
  <p class="section-text">Be specific but not rigid. "Warm acoustic folk song with fingerpicked guitar, soft female vocals, about finding home after being lost, recorded on tape" gives the AI enough direction to work with while leaving room for creative interpretation.</p>
  <p class="section-text">For sound design, describe what you hear in your head. "The sound of a massive stone door slowly grinding open in an ancient temple, with dust and echo" will give you something usable. The more cinematic your description, the better the output.</p>
</div>

<div class="demo-container">
  <h3>Music Prompt Formula</h3>
  <p><strong>Structure:</strong> [Genre] + [Mood] + [Instruments] + [Production Style]</p>
  <p><strong>Example 1:</strong> Cinematic orchestral, building tension, strings and brass, Hans Zimmer style</p>
  <p><strong>Example 2:</strong> Lo-fi jazz, late night, piano and soft drums, vinyl crackle</p>
  <p><strong>Example 3:</strong> Electronic ambient, ethereal and vast, synthesizer pads, reverb-heavy</p>
</div>

<div class="try-it-box">
  <h3>Try It: Generate a Soundtrack</h3>
  <p>Go to <strong>Suno.com</strong> and generate three tracks for a fictional short film. Use these prompts as starting points:</p>
  <div class="prompt-box"><code>Track 1 (Opening): Ambient electronic, mysterious and hopeful, soft synth pads with distant piano, sunrise energy

Track 2 (Conflict): Dark orchestral, tense and building, low strings with percussion hits, heartbeat rhythm

Track 3 (Resolution): Acoustic folk, warm and peaceful, fingerpicked guitar with cello, golden hour feeling</code></div>
  <p>Listen to how each track carries different emotional weight. Now imagine them in sequence. You just scored a film without touching an instrument.</p>
</div>
