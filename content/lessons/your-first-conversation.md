## narration
Your first conversation with Claude does not need to be complicated. In fact, the best way to start is simple. Just talk to it like you would talk to a smart colleague. No special syntax. No magic words. Just clear, natural language.
highlight: simple, colleague, clear, natural language

## narration
Let us start with the basics. Open Claude and type something like: Explain how solar panels work in simple terms. Claude will respond with a clear, structured explanation. No jargon unless you ask for it.
highlight: basics, Claude, explain, clear, structured

## narration
Now try something more useful. Give Claude a task from your real life. Write a professional email declining a meeting, but keep it friendly. Notice how Claude handles tone and nuance. It does not just generate text — it thinks about the social context.
highlight: task, real life, professional email, tone, nuance

## narration
Here is a pro tip — you can have a conversation. Claude remembers everything in the current chat. So you can say: Make it shorter. Add a line about rescheduling. Change the tone to more casual. Each message builds on the last.
highlight: conversation, remembers, builds on the last

## narration
What about when Claude gets it wrong? It happens. The best move is to course-correct in the same conversation. Say: That is not quite right. I meant this. Claude learns from your feedback within the conversation and adjusts immediately.
highlight: wrong, course-correct, feedback, adjusts
