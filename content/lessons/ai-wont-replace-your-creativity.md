## narration
Every time a new technology arrives, people panic. Photography will kill painting. Radio will kill live music. The internet will kill books. None of it happened. AI will not replace your creativity either. But it will change how you create — and in this lesson, you will learn why that is a good thing.

## narration
Here is what AI is genuinely good at in creative work. Generating variations fast. Exploring ideas you would never think of. Breaking through creative blocks. Handling the mechanical parts of production — resizing, reformatting, translating, adapting. AI is a production multiplier. It takes your one idea and gives you fifty versions to choose from.
highlight: variations, creative blocks, production multiplier, fifty versions

## narration
And here is what AI cannot do. It cannot feel. It cannot draw from lived experience. It has no taste — it does not know what is cliche versus what is fresh. It cannot make the bold, weird, personal choices that make creative work resonate. That is your job. You are the taste engine. AI is the production engine.
highlight: cannot feel, no taste, your job, taste engine, production engine

## narration
Real creatives are already using AI this way. Writers use it to brainstorm plot structures then write the actual prose themselves. Musicians use it to generate chord progressions then arrange and perform the music. Designers use it to explore visual directions then refine the winner by hand. The pattern is the same — AI generates, humans curate.
highlight: writers, musicians, designers, AI generates, humans curate

## narration
The creatives who struggle with AI are the ones who ask it to be creative for them. They type "write me a poem" and get something generic. Of course they do — they gave it no taste, no direction, no soul. The creatives who thrive are the ones who bring their vision and use AI to explore it faster than they ever could alone.
highlight: bring their vision, explore it faster, alone
