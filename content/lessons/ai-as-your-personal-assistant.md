## narration
The key mindset shift is this — stop asking AI single questions and start giving it ongoing context. Instead of "what should I eat tonight," say "I am trying to eat more protein, I have chicken, rice, and vegetables in the fridge, I have thirty minutes to cook, and I am tired. What should I make?" That extra context turns a generic answer into genuinely helpful advice.
highlight: ongoing context, extra context, genuinely helpful

## narration
AI excels at three categories of personal tasks. First, communication — drafting emails, texts, and messages in your tone. Second, planning — meal plans, travel itineraries, daily schedules, project breakdowns. Third, thinking — pros and cons lists, decision frameworks, brainstorming solutions to problems you are stuck on.
highlight: communication, planning, thinking

## narration
Here is a technique that changes everything — the context dump. At the start of your day, spend sixty seconds telling AI about your life. Your schedule. Your energy level. What is on your plate. Your goals for the week. Then ask it to help you plan. The output will shock you with how specific and useful it is.
highlight: context dump, sixty seconds, schedule, energy level, goals

## code: Daily Assistant Prompt
```
Good morning. Here's my situation today:
- Back-to-back meetings 9am to 1pm
- Need to finish the Q2 report by 5pm
- Feeling low energy (bad sleep)
- Picking up kids at 3:30
- Trying to exercise 3x this week

Help me plan my day realistically.
Prioritize the report. Suggest when to
exercise and what type given my energy.
```

## narration
The goal is not to outsource your life to AI. It is to offload the mental overhead — the planning, the organizing, the "what should I do next" paralysis. When AI handles the logistics, you have more brain space for the things that actually matter. More presence with your family. More focus on creative work. Less decision fatigue.
