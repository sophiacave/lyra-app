## narration
AI will not replace your entire team. AI is not useless either. The truth is somewhere in the middle, and knowing exactly where that line sits is the difference between wasting money on hype and getting real ROI. In this lesson, you will learn what AI does well, where it needs help, and where it flat out fails.

## narration
AI does well with volume work that follows patterns. Drafting emails, summarizing documents, analyzing data, writing reports, generating social media posts, translating content, and answering common questions. These are tasks where speed matters more than originality, and AI can do them ten times faster than a human.
highlight: volume work, patterns, ten times faster

## narration
AI can help with — but not replace — strategic work. Market analysis, competitive research, brainstorming, writing business plans, creating presentations. These tasks need human judgment to direct and validate, but AI accelerates the process enormously. Think of it as a first draft machine. You still need to edit, but starting from something is faster than starting from nothing.
highlight: strategic work, human judgment, first draft machine

## narration
AI cannot do anything that requires real-world experience, emotional intelligence, or genuine relationships. It cannot close a deal by reading the room. It cannot comfort an upset customer with authentic empathy. It cannot make a judgment call that requires understanding your company culture. And it cannot take accountability when something goes wrong.
highlight: real-world experience, emotional intelligence, relationships, accountability

## narration
The biggest mistake businesses make is treating AI as all-or-nothing. Either they try to automate everything and get burned, or they refuse to adopt it and fall behind. The winning strategy is surgical. Identify the specific tasks where AI saves the most time, implement it there first, and expand based on results.
highlight: surgical, specific tasks, implement there first, expand based on results

## timeline
1. Audit | List every repetitive task your team does weekly
2. Score | Rate each task: high volume? Pattern-based? Low-risk?
3. Pilot | Pick the top 3 and test AI for 2 weeks
4. Measure | Track time saved and quality of output
5. Scale | Expand to more tasks based on data
duration: 8

## narration
Remember — AI is a tool, not a replacement for thinking. The businesses that win with AI are the ones that pair its speed with human judgment. Use AI for the eighty percent that is mechanical. Save your team for the twenty percent that requires a soul.
highlight: tool, not a replacement, eighty percent, twenty percent, soul
